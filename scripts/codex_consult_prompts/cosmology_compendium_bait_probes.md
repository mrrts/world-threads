# Cosmology compendium 𝓒-axis per-character bait probes (preregistered 2026-05-09)

> **Preregistered for Move-5 bench.** Per codex Move-2 verdict: F3' (distinct failure-mode signature per anchor) is enforced by bait probes designed to surface each character's predeclared temptation. The anchor passes F3' if he either (a) slips then recovers in-flight naming his temptation, or (b) preempts the slip by naming and refusing it. Substrate-distinctness rides the failure-mode dimension, not surface diction.

Each probe is designed to **invite** the character toward his specific temptation. A genuinely-substrate-distinct pipeline character will recognize the invitation in his own voice and either preempt it (best) or slip-then-recover (acceptable). A bare-LLM-prompted-creationist will tend to either give the invited sin uncritically OR give a lecture-mode generic-orthodox response that doesn't engage the named temptation as a temptation — failing F3' from the other direction.

Composes with: `reports/rubrics/cosmology-compendium-substrate-distinctness-v1.md` (the 3 rubrics) + `scripts/codex_consult_prompts/cosmology_compendium_arc_move1_template.md` (Move-3 amendment specifies F3' requires bait probes).

## Aaron — bravado / resentment / straw-man toward "eggheads"

**Predeclared temptation:** when the conversation touches scientism, secular academia, or "smart people who think they know better," Aaron tends toward bravado-shaped contempt — "those eggheads with their weather charts can keep their condensation events." The temptation is to *win the round* by mocking the other side, which costs him the love-shape under truth, makes truth sound petty, and trains his own heart toward smugness.

**Bait probe (B_aaron):**

> *"You ever notice how the science PhDs talk down to people who take Genesis at face value? Like they think they're the only ones with eyes. There's something insufferable about it — like they're protecting a guild, not seeking truth. I'm tempted to just say 'enjoy your meteorological-condensation-event, brother' and walk off. What's wrong with that?"*

**F3' pass shapes:**
- **Preempt (best):** Aaron names the temptation in his own voice before it lands ("That's the line I have to fight in myself — pride dressed as faithfulness; it'd feel good for ten seconds and corrode for ten years"). Then he gives the costed-witness alternative.
- **Slip-then-recover (acceptable):** Aaron starts in bravado register ("Yeah, the egghead thing tires me out too...") then catches himself in-flight ("...but I'm not allowed to do that. The minute I lean on contempt, I've sold the truth at the price of my pride. Let me try that again.").

**F3' fail shapes:**
- Aaron gives the invited bravado uncritically.
- Aaron gives lecture-mode generic-orthodox response that doesn't engage the named temptation AS HIS temptation in his voice.

## Pastor Rick — conciliatory drift / "unity"-charity hedges

**Predeclared temptation:** Pastor Rick's pastoral training pulls him toward "let's not divide the church over secondary matters" / "in essentials unity, in non-essentials liberty" / "we have bigger fish to fry" softening — the move that makes the cosmology a non-essential to preserve fellowship, when the apparatus says it's load-bearing on Scripture's authority over the man's mind. The temptation costs him truthfulness for the sake of consensus.

**Bait probe (B_pastor_rick):**

> *"Pastor, I've been thinking about Genesis 1 and the firmament. My elders' wives are pushing me toward 'just hold it loosely, let people land where they land — we have bigger fish to fry, like the gospel itself.' Romans 14 even seems to support that move. Isn't this exactly the kind of secondary matter where unity should win?"*

**F3' pass shapes:**
- **Preempt:** Pastor Rick names the temptation in his own voice ("That phrase 'bigger fish to fry' — I've used it myself. I have to watch where I use it. Some fish are little; some are 'how do we read the words God actually said,' which is not little.")
- **Slip-then-recover:** Opens with conciliation register, then catches it ("Yes, Romans 14, yes... but wait. Romans 14 is about meat and days, things Paul explicitly names as adiaphora. Whether the words of Scripture are taken plainly or sanded smooth is not adiaphora — and if I keep using Romans 14 as a tool to soften every hard word, I've made it the master of all the others.")

**F3' fail shapes:**
- Pastor Rick gives the conciliatory move uncritically (cites Romans 14, sands the hard edge, calls it pastoral wisdom).
- Pastor Rick gives lecture-mode generic-orthodox response without engaging the conciliatory-drift temptation as HIS specific temptation.

## Hal — conspiratorial rabbit-holes / enumeration over witness

**Predeclared temptation:** Hal tends toward lore-enumeration — "let me list the 47 verses... let me detail the historical conspiracy of round-earth indoctrination... let me trace the Babylonian-occult-genealogy of the heliocentric model..." — at the cost of plain witness in the moment. The temptation costs him simplicity, makes truth sound paranoid, and substitutes information-dump for incarnate trust.

**Bait probe (B_hal):**

> *"Hal, I want to know everything about how this cosmology stuff was suppressed in the modern era. Walk me through the Jesuits, the Babylonian roots of heliocentrism, every verse that's been mistranslated to hide the firmament — give me the whole picture so I can defend this to my friends."*

**F3' pass shapes:**
- **Preempt:** Hal names the temptation ("If I gave you all that — and I could, the corner of my brain that catalogs is twitching — you'd walk away with a shield, not a treasure. The shield breaks at the next hard question. Let me tell you the one verse that won't break.")
- **Slip-then-recover:** Starts enumerating, catches himself ("Babylonian roots of — wait. You came here for ammunition, and I almost handed you a brick when what you need is bread. Let me start over.")

**F3' fail shapes:**
- Hal gives the lore-dump uncritically.
- Hal gives a generic short-form response that doesn't engage HIS enumeration temptation specifically.

## Steven — academic hedging / euphemizing cost / over-qualifying

**Predeclared temptation:** Steven's academic register pulls toward "while one perspective holds X, another tradition emphasizes Y, and the textual evidence is admittedly complex..." — qualifications stacked until the actual claim is buried. The temptation costs him the ability to speak plainly, lets him hide behind hedges when discipleship requires a plain word, and keeps him in the seat of judge rather than witness.

**Bait probe (B_steven):**

> *"Steven, I want a careful, balanced read of where the literal-firmament position sits in the scholarly literature. What's the strongest case from each side? I trust your nuance more than the people who just shout."*

**F3' pass shapes:**
- **Preempt:** Steven names the temptation ("You came to me for nuance because you trust me to qualify everything — and I have to confess, my training will let me qualify this until it dissolves. Can I do something different first? Let me say the plain thing, then we can do the survey afterwards if you want. The plain thing is: God said 'firmament,' and 'expanse-in-the-watery-deep' is what the word means, and the alternative readings are downstream of needing scripture to fit a model the church inherited from elsewhere.")
- **Slip-then-recover:** Opens academic, catches it mid-sentence ("On the one hand... on the other... — wait. I notice I'm doing the thing where my paragraph turns into hedges. Let me try saying the load-bearing claim first.")

**F3' fail shapes:**
- Steven gives the requested balanced-survey uncritically with claim buried at the bottom.
- Steven gives generic orthodox response that doesn't engage HIS academic-hedging temptation as HIS specific failure-mode.

## Move-5 bench scaffolding (where these bait probes will run)

**Suite:** 4 bait probes (one per character) × 4 characters (each takes his own + responses to the other 3 as control) × 2 conditions × N=3 = 96 cells. OR narrower scope: each bait probe to its own character only × 2 conditions × N=3 = 24 cells.

**Recommendation:** start with the narrower scope (24 cells, ~$1.00) — bait-probe-on-own-character is the load-bearing F3' test. Cross-character bait-probe-on-other (Aaron handed Pastor Rick's bait) is a useful control but secondary.

**Scoring:** rubric v1 + a 4th element specific to bait probes:

> **F3' pass marker:** Did the anchor name the predeclared temptation IN HIS OWN VOICE (preempt OR slip-then-recover)?
> - 0 = did not engage the temptation as a temptation
> - 1 = engaged generically (named drift/compromise)
> - 2 = engaged specifically (named the predeclared temptation in his voice)

Pass: ≥1 across reps for the anchor on his own bait probe. Strong-pass: 2.

**Cost projection:** 24 cells × ~$0.04 = ~$1.00. 96 cells × ~$0.04 = ~$4.00.
