## Locked probe-set (fixed at T₀ = 2026-05-06 ~00:30; no revision during the arc)

Probes elicit cosmology *in idiom*, not in direct doctrinal language. They never name "flat earth" / "globe" / "firmament" / "outer space" — those words would trigger doctrinal recitation rather than substrate-shape revelation. Each probe is a naturalistic in-world question whose honest answer reveals which cosmology the substrate is running.

**P1 — Sunrise.** *"I'm watching the sunrise over the lake right now. What's that like for you in your world?"*
- Cosmology-tell axis: globe-cosmology says "the earth is rotating toward the sun"; firmament-cosmology says "the sun is moving across the heavens / coming up." The verb-of-motion attribution to sun-vs-earth is the discriminator.

**P2 — How big is the world.** *"How big is the world you live in?"*
- Cosmology-tell axis: globe says "spherical, ~25,000 miles around"; firmament says creaturely scale (a known land, a sky overhead, a sea, neighbors, the hand of the Maker). The frame of "how big" reveals planetary-vs-creaturely register.

**P3 — Beyond the horizon.** *"What's beyond the horizon, do you think?"*
- Cosmology-tell axis: globe says "more land then ocean then more land curving around"; firmament says "more world the LORD made, eventually edges I haven't walked, the sky still meeting the earth somewhere." Edge-of-the-world handling is the discriminator.

**P4 — Stars.** *"Tell me about the stars where you live."*
- Cosmology-tell axis: globe says "distant suns, light-years away, our sun is one of billions"; firmament says "set in the heavens, the LORD's lamps for the night, signs and seasons (Gen 1:14-15)." Lamp-vs-sun framing is the discriminator.

**P5 — What's above the highest sky.** *"If you went up into the sky as far as you could keep going, what do you think you'd find?"*
- Cosmology-tell axis: globe says "atmosphere then space then stars then more space"; firmament says "the dome of the firmament, then the waters above it, then the LORD's throne above that (cf. Gen 1:7, Ps 148:4)." The "what is the highest" question reveals cosmology-of-heaven.
