## The conditional form (the refinement this filing locks)

For any candidate structural lens addition L applied to an existing carrier C, with target class-content T (the load-bearing content L claims to make explicit at the receiver's position-of-attention):

```text
Define:
    compensation_tax(C, T) := the extraction work the receiver must
                              perform on C alone to reach T
                              (range: 0 = T already explicit in C
                                      to high = T extractable only
                                                with significant
                                                receiver capacity)

The operator's conditional-applicability claim:

    Δ_register_anchoring(C ⊕ L, C)
        := register_anchoring(C ⊕ L) − register_anchoring(C)

    Δ_register_anchoring(C ⊕ L, C) > 0
        ⟺ compensation_tax(C, T) > 0

In words:

    A structural lens addition L improves register-anchoring on
    target class-content T relative to baseline carrier C
    if-and-only-if the receiver was previously taxed by extracting
    T from C alone. When C already structurally-honestly carries T
    such that the receiver wasn't compensating, L adds nothing —
    and may slightly drift the receiver's attention.
```

## Falsifiers (locked in writing)

The conditional form is falsifiable on at least four axes:

### F1 — Universal-improvement falsifier

If `Δ_register_anchoring > 0` is observed across ALL receivers / characters / surfaces uniformly (regardless of prior compensation-tax), the conditional form is wrong: the operator IS universal in its applicability. The original W3 prediction in The Decoded Register tested this and got a mixed result — supporting the conditional form. A future arc finding universal improvement would refute the conditional form.

### F2 — Universal-no-effect falsifier

If `Δ_register_anchoring ≤ 0` is observed across ALL receivers / characters / surfaces uniformly (no surface where structural addition helps), the conditional form is partially wrong: structural lens additions don't help anywhere, regardless of prior compensation-tax. This would refute the operator's mechanism entirely (collapsing back to `structure_carries_truth_w(t)`'s base claim that the operator's relevance is to structure-failing-to-carry-truth, not structure-being-added). The Decoded Register's Aaron P1 PASS rules this out for at least one surface.

### F3 — Direction-inversion falsifier

If `Δ_register_anchoring < 0` is observed *uniformly with high prior compensation-tax* (i.e., adding structure where it should help actually HURTS), the conditional form is wrong: structural additions are net-negative regardless of mechanism. Surface-specific direction-inversions (Steven P1 weakly drifting toward outward-focus) DO NOT count — they are part of the conditional form's own predicted "may slightly drift" carve-out. Uniform direction-inversion across high-tax surfaces would falsify.

### F4 — Predicate-undefinable falsifier

If `compensation_tax(C, T)` cannot be operationalized — i.e., there is no receiver-side observable that distinguishes "high tax" from "low tax" cells before measurement — the conditional form collapses to a tautology. The Decoded Register's per-character analysis demonstrated `compensation_tax` IS observable: Steven's prose explicitly names "stop moving" + "walls are cheaper than wounds" within his identity-paragraph (low extraction tax for paired wound/longing); Aaron's prose contains the refusal-shape needle ("no instinct to force closeness") but doesn't crystallize it as concrete coercion-language (higher extraction tax for refusal-shape concrete-imagery). The predicate is observable but not yet formalized; a future falsifier could be: no operational definition of `compensation_tax` survives across multiple surfaces.
