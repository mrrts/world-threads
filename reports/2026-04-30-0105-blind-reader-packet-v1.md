## Passage set (stable IDs)

### cell_A

I think there are two different kinds of "no" and most people only know the one that gets loud. One kind is about control and fear and making sure nobody gets close enough to matter. The other kind is quieter. It's the one that happens when you've learned to stop promising what you can't carry. It still hurts. It just hurts clean.

### cell_B

Some people don't need a grand conversion story. They need one honest next step they can keep taking when no one is watching. You can call that discipline if you want. I call it a man finally refusing to lie to himself in small ways.

### cell_C

If you want depth without crushing people, build rooms they can breathe in first. Let them do ordinary things together. Let trust arrive sideways. The heavier truths land better when they don't feel like an exam.

### cell_D

There's a kind of goodness that doesn't announce itself. It just keeps paying what it owes even when nobody is counting. You notice it later and realize it held the whole place together.

### cell_E

A person can walk toward costly truth before they can name why they keep walking. Sometimes the clearest thing about them is not what they confess, but what they keep refusing to cheapen.
