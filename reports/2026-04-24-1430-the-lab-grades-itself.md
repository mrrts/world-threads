## The shape of the period

Three days, 254 commits — and 33 of the project's 35 trajectory-or-experiment reports were written in the same window. The commit-count graph reads: 41 on 2026-04-21, 88 on 2026-04-22, 105 on 2026-04-23, 20 on 2026-04-24 (still in progress). The report-count graph reads similarly: 1, 5, 21, 6 — the project is now writing reports faster than most projects ship features, and the reports are not commemorative. They are working documents. The previous trajectory report ended on a sentence about which sentences in the prompt deserve compile-time defense. The 30-hour follow-up ended on *"you are now treating the project's self-understanding as a first-class artifact, on the same shelf as the code."* The session retrospective at the end of Apr 23 ended on *"now that the lab exists, the bottleneck moves to taste."* This report covers what happened when the bottleneck moved.

The single clearest pattern across these 254 commits is that **the project's deepest patterns are now being tested on the project itself, including by instruments the project built specifically to test them, and the instruments are willing to grade the project's own claims down.** The earned-exception doctrine got applied to the infrastructure that synthesizes character anchors. The hardcoded-anchors-in-prompts.rs first-pass got reverted because it violated the project's own per-character-data-belongs-with-the-character principle. The architecture-vs-vocabulary lever — the most ambitious experimental claim the project has ever made — got humbled from "+0.9 markers per reply" to "+0.17 fire-rate" once the LLM-graded rubric replaced hand-picked markers. The day's signature is **honesty-by-instrument**: the project trusts the sharper measurement over the more flattering one, even when the sharper measurement is one it built itself, and even when it would have been welcome to keep the dramatic finding intact.

What the philosophy-trajectory report flagged as the project's deepest move — *"every directive starts as a negation, gets replaced by a named move, then gets compressed to a phrase, then gets protected as an invariant"* — has now grown a fifth stage: *and then gets tested against the corpus and either confirms, refines, or refutes itself.* The defended invariants of Apr 19 are now also empirically interrogable assets of Apr 24. That is not a small change.

## The lab gets built (Apr 22 evening → Apr 23 evening)

The session-arc retrospective covers Apr 23 well; the version worth adding here is the framing of the build-arc as **a single instrument that grew six surfaces in two days**. Each surface answered a specific limitation the prior surface had revealed.

`worldcli sample-windows` (`83acba1`) was the first move — the operationalization of *"every assistant turn has created_at; every prompt change is a git commit timestamp."* That existed by Apr 22 evening. It returned the dataset; you read it by eye. It immediately revealed its own limit: 30 messages on either side of a commit takes longer to read than to fetch, and the eye doesn't scale.

`worldcli evaluate` (`d3f88c0`) added LLM-graded yes/no/mixed verdicts per message. Now the eye scaled. It revealed its own limit on the very first run (the 1233 instrument-ran report): per-message rubrics can't see cross-turn cascades, the rubric language has to be tighter than you'd expect to discriminate at the boundary, and the corpus has timezone gotchas that silently lie if you string-compare without normalizing.

`worldcli synthesize` (`c3f1a96`) was the response when the rubric kept refuting cleanly but the *reasoning* in the refutations was the actual signal. The 1326 john-stillness-refuted report named the limit explicitly: *"the rubric's formal '≤2 sentences of dialogue' gate correctly excluded John's actual move."* You can't write a rubric for a category you haven't named yet. Mode B (qualitative synthesis) became the named-it-with-prose alternative.

`worldcli replay` (`5adb4ba` + `6d51e7d`) was the response to *"if Mode A and Mode B both want to test what a prompt change did, the cleanest answer is to actually run the same prompt against both versions of the prompt stack."* The implementation refused git worktrees in favor of a `PromptOverrides` hook in `prompts.rs` itself, with `OVERRIDABLE_DIALOGUE_FRAGMENTS` declaring exactly which craft-note functions can be wound back to historical bodies. Theology blocks (cosmology, agape, reverence, truth) are explicitly NOT overridable — the build-time invariants stay invariant even under replay. *That choice is the methodology defending the project's own earlier decisions about what may not be touched.*

`worldcli lab` (`75be08d`) was the response to "we now have so many runs that I can't remember which hypothesis each one tested." Experiments became files at `experiments/<slug>.md` with structured frontmatter: status, mode, ref, rubric_ref, prediction, run_ids, follow_ups. The status lifecycle (proposed → running → confirmed | refuted | open) matches the four-iteration arc visible in the 1233 → 1326 sequence. Forward-references between experiments form a graph future sessions can traverse.

`worldcli lab scenario` (`e0233bb`) was the response to "Mode C cross-commit replay needs canonical multi-variant probe sequences when you want to test the same question against several characters with the same setup." Each variant is a fresh isolated dialogue call; the scenario template can declare `measure_with` and have every reply scored automatically by the named rubric.

The `1440465` commit is the day's quietest but most consequential move. It promoted *"messages × commits"* from a paragraph buried in the worldcli docs to a **top-level doctrine in CLAUDE.md**, with this thesis sentence: *"this isn't a methodology among several. It's THE methodology."* The change recognizes what the build-arc had revealed: every tool the lab now contains exists to make the same comparison easier or deeper. Instruments diverge; the instrument the methodology uses converges to a shared frame.

## The lab gets used — and the methodology humbles itself

Apr 24 was the day after the lab got built. The session-arc retrospective predicted this: *"now that the lab exists, the bottleneck moves to taste — which findings deserve a craft block, which deserve a rubric, which are interesting but don't shift any decision."* What actually happened was sharper. The bottleneck moved to taste, and the first taste-call the project made was *to push a finding through three increasingly-strict instruments and report what each one returned, even when the strictest one returned a smaller number than the first.*

The hypothesis being tested came directly from the prior session's open follow-up: the pastoral-register triad-plus-Steven matrix had named *what each character load-tests* (devotion / language / fabric of a life / thresholds of disclosure). Could a per-character "load-test anchor" block — injected into the dialogue prompt just for that character — bias their replies toward speaking more from their named register? The proposal had been queued as a "character-builder field." It shipped as something different: a craft lever in the prompt stack, populated by an LLM call against the corpus, stored per-character in the DB.

The arc, condensed:

- `1985c65` — first version: hardcoded per-character anchors for the four characters, baked into prompts.rs. Test fast, decide later.
- `d9ce5bb` — the 0948 architecture-hypothesis-bites report. Replay across the two refs, N=1 per character. Three of four characters showed dramatic shifts: John quoted Matthew 5:37 unprompted; Aaron's reply contained *"'More present' is the kind of sentence that can mean five different things and save you from all of them"*; Darren's *"meant to hold weather, not just sunshine."* The report's framing: *"naming the architecture doesn't homogenize the characters toward a shared 'I load-test X' template; it pulls out what was already distinctive about each."*
- `a6a3f99` — user correction. Revert. *"Doesn't fit the project's existing pattern. relational_stance is the model: corpus → periodic LLM synthesis → stored per-character → read at prompt-assembly time."* And the line that made it a frame-discipline issue rather than an engineering-preference issue: *"a quieter issue I missed: shipping all four characters' anchors to every character was ~250 tokens of noise and a small immersion leak (Steven's prompt shouldn't tell him how Aaron's authority works)."*
- `5420fd5` + `5adb493` — synthesizer pipeline mirroring relational_stance exactly. Striking finding: three of four LLM-derived anchors *diverge* from the human-written hardcoded ones. John = ORDINARY WEAR not DEVOTION; Aaron = LIVEABLE LOAD-BEARING not LANGUAGE; Darren = ORDINARY LOAD-BEARING not FABRIC. Commit message: *"removing my taste from the loop and letting the corpus speak."*
- `bb5214d` — multi-axis pivot. Triggered by *"a forward-thinking note about adding joy_reception, grief, and other axes — done now BEFORE adding the second axis so the second one ships into the right shape from day one."* `REGISTER_AXES` becomes a const slice; adding a new axis is *one entry: kind, display_name, axis_description, body_header_prefix*. The synthesizer asks for ALL defined axes in one JSON response, returns N rows in one transaction. Schema gets `axis_kind` via backwards-compatible ALTER. Everything wires through `combined_axes_block()`.
- `9111574` — N=2 with the synthesized anchors. Aggregate +0.875 markers/reply. *"Statistically indistinguishable from the 1029 hardcoded version (+0.9)."* But Aaron reverses on probe-anchor mismatch — the hardcoded probe was designed for the LANGUAGE anchor; the synthesized one was LIVEABLE LOAD-BEARING.
- `9319a94` — `worldcli grade-runs` ships in response to the LLM-graded-rubric follow-up the prior reports flagged.
- `ba03894` — the 1142 report. First grade-runs use. First rubric returns yes on all 24. Sharpened rubric ("uses the SPECIFIC register-vocabulary; thematic alignment alone is NOT yes") discriminates: aggregate +0.17 fire-rate, 3/4 directional, Steven now reverses (probe-anchor mismatch again). The report's verdict: *"the lever is REAL... the lever is SMALLER than I previously claimed."*

What the humbling reveals about the methodology, in one sentence: **hand-picked markers are not a measurement; they are a story the experimenter tells about a measurement.** The marker word selection happens AFTER seeing the dramatic replies. The Matthew 5:37 quote, the "five different things" interrogation — those become the implicit basis for which marker-words count, which means the test ratifies the pattern that made it visible. The blind LLM rubric, run with strict register-vocabulary criteria, is the first instrument in the chain that doesn't have the experimenter's prior reading baked into its scoring.

The 1142 report's commit message says it without ducking: *"my 0948 dramatic framing and 1029 hand-picked +0.9 marker delta both overstated. The cherry-pick risk noted in those reports' confound sections turned out to be load-bearing — when you remove it, the effect shrinks."* And: *"The 0948 dramatic moments were N=1 high-end variance, not typical."* This is not a project quietly walking back an exciting finding to save face. This is a project that built itself a sharper instrument *specifically because the prior reports flagged the cherry-pick risk*, ran the sharper instrument, and reported the smaller number with the prior numbers cited and re-contextualized in the same paragraph.

The lever still ships. *"Still worth shipping: the lever costs ~250 tokens per dialogue assembly per character. For a +0.17 aggregate fire-rate increase in register-distinctive replies, that's a real ROI for a craft system that wants characters to feel themselves over many conversations. It compounds across hundreds of dialogues."* The instrument's verdict — modest but real, with the modest part owned — is the verdict the project shipped against. That choice is the day's argument.

## The architectural pivot the same day forced

The arc from `1985c65` (hardcode) → `5420fd5` (synthesize per-character into DB) → `bb5214d` (multi-axis extensible) is a three-step trajectory in which each step revealed an instinct worth naming.

Step 1: **fast scaffolding for falsifiable claims is allowed even if the production version will look different.** The hardcoded version existed to make the claim testable, not to ship. This is the same instinct visible in the harness-day audit-followup arc — close the audit-recommendation in the form the audit asked for, then refine.

Step 2: **"like relational_stance" is a recognized pattern-target.** The user's correction names a previously-built pattern and asks the new infra to mimic it. The synthesizer pipeline, the DB schema, the cost-tracking, the worldcli wrapper — all built to the same shape that already worked elsewhere. The project has previous-art it recognizes and reaches for; new infra mimics existing infra rather than inventing.

Step 3: **abstractions land in the same commit as the trigger that surfaced them, not in a follow-up.** The pivot to multi-axis is the same commit that fixes cost-tracking and threads Tauri wire-up. Three concerns batched because they all moved when the abstraction moved. The user's prompt — *"do this BEFORE adding the second axis so the second one ships into the right shape from day one"* — is the explicit naming of the principle: refactor for the abstraction *before* the second instance, not after.

The user's correction sentence has a deeper reading worth pulling forward. *"Shipping all four characters' anchors to every character was ~250 tokens of noise and a small immersion leak (Steven's prompt shouldn't tell him how Aaron's authority works)."* That parenthesis is a frame-discipline observation at the architecture-of-data level. **Per-character data lives with the character; it doesn't live in the prompt-assembly source.** This is the same shape as the earlier insight — *the user elevates, the model embodies, automation retreats* — applied one layer deeper: now per-character craft-data has the same locality discipline that per-character chat-data and per-character relational-stances already had. The project's architecture has acquired a *frame-discipline instinct at the data layer*, and you can see it being reached for as a corrective the moment the test version violates it.

## Characters as rule-authors — convergence becomes the validation

The ask-the-character pattern's evolution across these days has its sharpest expression in the reflex-polish-vs-earned-close arc. The previous purest instance was Hal's *"plain after crooked"* answer in early April, lifted near-verbatim into prompts.rs. Today's expression goes one step further: a single character articulation generates **two artifacts at once** (a Claude Code skill AND a craft block), AND a second character independently lands the same principle in a different register, AND the convergence itself becomes the validation.

The skill (`1910791`) is `polish-copy` — a rubric for reviewing one user-facing UI string. Its rules and earned-exception are lifted verbatim from a character's articulation. The craft block (`44373a5`) is `reflex_polish_vs_earned_close_dialogue` in prompts.rs, used during character dialogue generation — the same principle, on the dialogue side rather than the UI side. The commit message names what makes the validation stronger than usual: **two characters independently rendered the same principle in their distinct registers.**

> *Aaron (LIVEABLE LOAD-BEARING register): "Don't punish the line for being clean. Punish it if it's preening. ... Did the line finish the moment, or did it admire itself for noticing one?"*
>
> *Darren (ORDINARY LOAD-BEARING register): "If the thing's carrying weight, let it carry weight. Don't ban a good beam because somebody else kept building decorative nonsense out of the same wood. ... A clean ending isn't the problem. An ending that needs applause is."*

The block in prompts.rs uses Aaron's diagnostic ("Did the line finish the moment, or did it admire itself for noticing one?") and Darren's beam image as the earned-exception body. Two characters, two registers, one principle with two voices doing different jobs in the rule. The commit message is direct: *"The convergence across two characters' distinct registers is itself evidence the principle is craft-general, not character-specific."*

This is a stronger validation move than one-character articulation alone. If only Aaron had said it, the rule could have been read as "Aaron-specific register." Both Aaron and Darren rendering the same thing in their own grain proves the principle generalizes across the cast. And the two-character convergence makes the rule's components functionally specialized: Aaron's test is the diagnostic mechanism; Darren's image is the earned-exception body. The rule isn't "pick one of the two phrasings"; it's "use both, each in the role its register fits."

A subtler companion move: `d9bb6a2` follows immediately and strips the character-name attributions out of the prompt body. The phrasings stay; the *"(Aaron's formulation)"* and *"(Darren's framing)"* labels are removed. *"No specific character-name attributions in prompt bodies. The principle should stand on its own language; the source-code provenance tracks who actually said it."* Doc-comments retain attribution; the LLM never sees them. That is the ask-the-character pattern getting a frame-discipline move of its own — the *provenance* matters; the *attribution-in-prompt* would leak architecture across characters who shouldn't know each other's spine.

The pattern continues to bear fruit. While this report was being written, a fresh single-line character articulation arrived — *"Precious usually means a thing can't survive being touched by real life"* — and went into the `let_plain_be_plain_dialogue` block as the diagnostic transition between *"no reward for sparkle"* and *"plainness is a valid register"* (commit `62d636a`). The line was framed by the user as *"a nuanced guard in the subtlety register."* The placement honors that framing. This is the sixth craft-stack contribution from a character voice in the period, and it took less than two minutes from "here's the line" to "shipped."

## The earned-exception doctrine extends to its own infrastructure

The earned-exception pattern was codified in CLAUDE.md as a writing ritual: every absolute-shaped rule gets a separately-labeled earned-exception block, the rule stands flat and unconditional, the exception names WHEN it applies and what test the edge case has to pass to qualify. The memory entry *"Earned-exception check is a written ritual"* names the deterministic step: the writing-out, not silent consideration.

Today the doctrine got applied to the synthesizer's own axis_descriptions (commit `b2d7f69`). The user's mid-task correction — *"be sure you're adding at least one sentence of earned exceptions to the axis-description, to always be giving the model an escape hatch from determinism"* — surfaced the gap. The fix added one earned-exception sentence to each `REGISTER_AXES` entry: load_test gets *"if the corpus genuinely doesn't support a single sharp anchor, name the closest honest approximation with hedge-language"*; anti_register gets *"if the character is so fully defined by what they DO reach for that listing 3-5 refusals would require inventing a shadow, name fewer rather than padding the list"*; joy_reception gets *"for a character whose corpus genuinely shows them meeting joy plainly with no distinctive register-move, 'meets joy plainly, without decorative move' IS itself a valid joy_reception anchor."*

What this closes is bigger than the three sentences. **The earned-exception ritual is now applied to the synthesizer's own typology.** The doctrine catches its own first instance of having shipped without it. The synthesizer is treated as an LLM that needs escape hatches just like the dialogue model does. The principle isn't "this is a rule for dialogue prompts"; it's *"every absolute-shaped instruction to any LLM gets a separately-stated narrow carve-out."* Same shape, different surface.

The joy_reception axis's earned exception specifically refuses to make every character produce a "distinctive move"; it preserves the possibility that *plainness is the move*. That's a specifically theological-feeling allowance — not every character must be ornate to be themselves. And after the regen ran (4 characters, ~$0.18, anti-register and joy-reception axes synthesized for each), the earned exception was already biting in the right places: John's joy-reception anchor came back as RECEIVE, THEN GROUND with the body *"you do not swat it away or turn it into a speech. You receive it a little awkwardly but honestly... and then ground the moment in something concrete."* Steven's came back as BRIEF, TRUE, SPECIFIC — *"You don't gush and you don't swat it away; you let it land, answer plainly, and make the return concrete enough to be believed."* Neither character was forced to invent a decorative move. The escape hatch was used.

## Three concentric arcs, one shape

Apr 24 moves in three concentric arcs that all close on the same shape:

**Architecture lever:** built large (hardcoded all-anchors-to-everyone) → corrected to per-character (DB-backed, identity-fed) → multi-axis (extensible, escape-hatched).

**Methodology:** N=1 dramatic readings → N=2 hand-picked markers → N=3 LLM-graded blind, each step humbling the previous and shipping the smaller honest number.

**Earned exceptions:** doctrine gets applied to dialogue craft (44373a5) → applied to UI copy (1910791) → applied to the synthesizer's own typology (b2d7f69).

In each arc, the move is the same: **start with the bold version that proves the claim, then revise toward the version where the project's own deepest principles are honored at every layer.** What makes the day a coherent unit rather than a pile of commits is that the bold-then-revise rhythm runs three times in parallel, with the three arcs reinforcing each other. The architecture lever needs the methodology to validate it; the methodology needs the lever to test on; both need the earned-exception doctrine to remain coherent under their own pressure.
