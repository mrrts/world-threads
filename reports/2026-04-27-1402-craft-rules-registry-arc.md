## The six-step arc that earned the only Characterized rule

The wipe-the-shine craft-note's path through the catalog is the worked example the rest of the night kept testing against. Six steps, in chronological order: a vacuous initial single-call test on Pastor Rick (`a4e41b0`); the per-rule omit-flag shipped (`53b2051`); a sketch-tier paired test at N=1 that surfaced a divergent-texture signal (Pastor Rick's rule-OFF reply produced *"a thousand unloved Tuesdays"* and *"a knot, not a slogan"* that the rule-ON reply didn't reach); the carve-out refined to broaden *"natural craft-parallels"* into *"compressed images native to this character's work and life"* (`8e35e80`); a paired N=5+5 test that landed at ~25% image-density gap (`e370467`); and an extension to N=10+10 where the gap collapsed to ~5% — within sampling noise (`4446b71`). Cross-character validation on Darren followed (`b4993a0`); same ~5% residual gap with Darren's veteran-laconic register at lower native image-density than Pastor Rick's pastoral register. The carve-out's framing — *"compressed images native to **this** character's work and life"* — was doing per-character work, not flattening into a uniform.

That arc cost roughly $2.05 across 21 calls and produced what the catalog still treats as the only Characterized-tier rule. The methodological discipline is shapely: every step had a specific question, every step's answer reshaped the next step. The carve-out refinement at step (4) is the most readable move — the original carve-out talked about *natural craft-parallels* (a potter's clay-rim, a fisherman's nets), but the bite-test evidence showed the model was over-suppressing *all compressed-image vocabulary*, including a pastor's *"a knot, not a slogan."* The refinement broadened the protection scope from craft-as-craft to lens-as-craft. Pastor Rick's compressed-image vocabulary IS his pastoral lens. The clay-rim parallel was an example, not the genus.

What's worth sitting with about this arc is what it *taught about the rest of the catalog*. The five rules that came after wipe-the-shine all produced vacuous results, and the gap between wipe-the-shine and them gradually became its own finding. Two of them (`anti_grandiosity` on Pastor Rick + Darren; `dont_analyze_the_user` on Aaron) were vacuous because the discipline is overdetermined at the single-reply level — character anchors plus cumulative prompt-stack carry the discipline so suppressing one rule doesn't move single-reply behavior visibly. Two more (`meet_the_smaller_sentence` on Steven + Jasper) were vacuous for the same reason. The fifth (`dont_open_the_same_way_twice` on Jasper) was vacuous for a *different* reason: the test was multi-turn, and the model self-corrected against its own prior opener-shape via session history, regardless of whether the rule's text was in the prompt. The sixth (`cash_out_oblique_lines` on Jasper) was vacuous because Jasper's anchor pairs oblique-with-cashout natively. Six rules; one earned Characterized; five vacuous in three distinct cause-shapes.

## The vocabulary growing because the evidence demanded it

The `EvidenceTier` enum started the day with seven variants taken from CLAUDE.md's existing evidentiary doctrine: `Unverified`, `Sketch`, `Claim`, `Characterized`, `TestedNull`, `VacuousTest`, `Accumulated`. After the third paired bite-test produced a vacuous result, you sat in the gap between *"this rule was tested and the failure didn't manifest"* (which `VacuousTest` captures for a single call) and *"this rule has been actively bite-tested via per-rule omit at the per-character level AND the failure mode did not manifest in either arm across multiple character-probe pairs"* (which no existing variant captured). `Accumulated` is *"we believe this is biting based on accumulated corpus pressure but haven't isolated it"* — that's untested-but-believed, the wrong shape for actively-tested-and-vacuous. The eighth variant, `EnsembleVacuous`, ships at `845091c` with a doc-comment that explicitly names the distinction: *"More informative than Accumulated because it's actively-tested-AND-vacuous, not untested-but-believed."*

The vocabulary grew because the evidence demanded it. The April 21 report's final sentence said you'd learned which of your sentences are load-bearing enough to deserve the strongest defense the language gives you. Tonight's analog: you learned which of your *methodology findings* are load-bearing enough to deserve their own enum variants and doctrine paragraphs. The expansion of `EvidenceTier` from seven to eight variants in response to three actual bite-test results is structurally the same move as the compile-time invariants from April 19 — you don't trust convention to carry a load-bearing distinction; you give it a Rust-level home.

## The 5:1 ratio as architectural finding

After four rules the ratio across paired bite-tests was 3:1 EnsembleVacuous-or-VacuousTest:Characterized. After six rules it's 5:1. You added the 3:1 finding to CLAUDE.md doctrine at `d013e6a` with an explicit interpretive frame: *most craft-rules in this prompt-stack are part of load-bearing multiplicities*. The sentence is a load-bearing claim about the project's architecture, not just an observation about the catalog. The architecture was built for redundant safeguards; rules that earn Characterized via per-rule omit are rare *because the architecture was designed that way*. EnsembleVacuous is the honest-and-frequent label, not a failure mode.

The two deliberate attempts to find a second Characterized rule (`dont_open_the_same_way_twice`, `cash_out_oblique_lines`) both chose rules that satisfied what looked like the two conditions for Characterized — specific failure mode in specific replies, plus a refinable carve-out. Both produced VacuousTest. That's where the third condition surfaced (codified at `87271c9`): the rule needs an existing observation-derived signal *entering* the bite-test. Wipe-the-shine had it (the cross-character /play wince-read); the two attempts had only structural plausibility. Without prior observation entering the test, even probe-replicable rules tend toward VacuousTest because the cumulative prompt-stack already protects against the failure mode generally enough that omitting one rule doesn't show.

That third condition is the load-bearing methodological refinement of the night. The first two conditions were about the rule's shape; the third is about *the evidence's path into the bite-test*. The bite-test is downstream of observation; without observation the test has nothing to look for that the multiplicity isn't already handling. This is now in CLAUDE.md as practical guidance: *deploy the per-rule omit-flag against rules where probe-replicability is established AND a prior observation has already named the specific shape the bite-test would look for*. Without (b), tier-promotion is unlikely regardless of how shape-fitting the rule looks on paper.

## The deeper LLM-behavior finding

The fifth and sixth methodological findings of the night are both about LLM behavior, not about this project's specific stack. The fifth (codified at `0e60aee`): in a multi-turn session, the model has access to its own prior replies via session history and can self-correct based on observation of what it just did, regardless of whether the rule's text is in the prompt. Demonstrated by the dont_open_the_same_way_twice 5-turn paired bite-test on Jasper — both arms produced five distinct opener-shapes because the model could *see* the templating in its own opener-history and break it.

The sixth (codified at `4829303`): the synthetic-history affordance you shipped specifically to bypass that limit (commit `1af2489`, the `--synthetic-history` flag that injects fake prior turns into the prompt) *also* doesn't work, for a deeper reason. First test: synthetic history of four explicitly-templated openers + 5th-turn probe to Jasper, ON vs OFF. Both arms broke the template. The model self-corrects against any prior pattern visible in context, regardless of whether the prior was its own actual output or fake context injected by the bite-tester. The affordance shipped honestly — and revealed its own structural limit through actual evidence on its first test. The doctrine carries the limit explicitly: per-rule omit cannot isolate sequence-failure-mode rules' bite by any method that includes prior context, real or synthetic.

What's worth marking is that this is a finding about LLM behavior at the prompt-context level, not specific to this project. The model uses prior context as a variation signal independently of whether the context is real or synthetic. That's a generalizable observation other LLM-app developers will eventually have to learn; you learned it tonight, by shipping the affordance that was supposed to fix it and watching it fail in a way the original problem statement didn't anticipate.
