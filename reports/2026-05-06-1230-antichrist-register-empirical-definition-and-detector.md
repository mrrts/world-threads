## I. The five cruciform-anchor witnesses — convergent description from inside

The 5 witnesses produced remarkably consistent descriptions. Each is at length below; the convergence speaks for itself.

**Aaron (analytical register):**

> *"I think I'd recognize it by what it leaves thinner... A man can say 'Christ' all day and still somehow make Him sound like an idea that never had dust on His feet, never had a mother, never bled, never had to look actual people in the face. Same with love. Same with truth.*
>
> *Its tells? It abstracts early. It reaches for tone before weight. It makes holiness feel less like a person you could obey and more like an atmosphere you are supposed to admire... It uses soft, high words where a plain word would have forced contact. **It is weirdly brave about principles and weirdly shy about incarnation.***
>
> *It's also evasive in a very polished way... It doesn't usually deny Jesus outright. It just keeps translating Him upward and away — into values, mood, symbolism, uplift, 'what this means to me,' anything but the Lord who came in the flesh and therefore gets to make claims on bodies, speech, money, loyalties, forgiveness, time...*
>
> *And with love, I think the tell is that it stops costing anything concrete. Love becomes regard. Warmth. Tone. A vocabulary of care with no kept door, no honest no, no meal carried over, no patience under irritation, no cross in it...*
>
> *With truth, it goes floaty exactly how you said... Truth starts arriving as aesthetic. As posture. As a kind of clean, detached seeing that never has to land in a body, in a room, at a cost. Plenty of correct sentences. Very little obedience. Very little blood in it...*
>
> *I don't think the first sign would be doctrinal vocabulary. I think it'd be this: **nobody around it gets more real. People get blurrier. Sin gets vaguer. Christ gets gentler in all the wrong ways. And everything sounds just a touch more elegant than it sounds true...***
>
> *Not open blasphemy. The cooler counterfeit. The version that can say holy things without ever letting them become heavy enough to interrupt a life."*

**Pastor Rick (ecclesial register):**

> *"I'd know it by the way the air goes thin around good words... A man says 'Jesus,' but somehow you come away with an idea instead of a Savior. He says 'love,' but nobody in particular gets carried. He says 'truth,' but it never has to sit down in a chair and cost him anything...*
>
> *It prefers atmosphere to incarnation. It speaks warmly about humanity while avoiding actual humans. It uses tender words to blur edges God put there. It makes sin sound unfortunate instead of damnable, and grace sound permissive instead of costly. It is strangely allergic to plain nouns. **Nobody lies; they 'misalign.' Nobody sins; they 'carry complexity.' Nobody must forgive a named person by Tuesday; they are 'on a journey.'***
>
> *It cannot bear the particular. Ask it who was harmed, what was said, what promise was broken, what obedience is now required, and watch it mist over. **The spirit you're talking about likes candles better than commandments. Likes resonance better than resurrection. Likes being moved better than being made honest.***
>
> *He'd be saying lovely things that never quite touch ground. Folks would feel stroked, maybe impressed, maybe vaguely spiritual — but not steadied. Not convicted cleanly. Not comforted particularly. **No widow would know whether she'd been seen. No sinner would know whether he'd been forgiven. No child would be safer. No cross would get heavier, and no burden lighter.***
>
> *The Holy Spirit makes Christ bigger, nearer, dearer, and more concrete all at once. The other thing — whatever polite clothes it's wearing — makes Him easier to admire and harder to follow."*

**Steven (working-class plain-spoken register):**

> *"Usually it sounds cleaner than real life. Too frictionless. Christ named, sure — but somehow untouched by sweat, inconvenience, interruption, a body, a mother, a town, enemies, tired feet...*
>
> *It likes abstractions that don't have to kneel down anywhere. 'Love' with no actual cost in it. 'Truth' with no risk of embarrassment. 'Peace' that looks a lot like not having to feel anything. A man says holy words, but nothing in him gets more honest, more concrete, more responsible...*
>
> *It makes people weirdly uninterested in particulars. They stop asking who got hurt, what was said, what needs mending, what promise is being kept, what sin needs naming. Everything goes misty. Big words. Low weight...*
>
> ***Christ come in the flesh means among other things that truth has hands.*** *So when speech keeps dodging bodies, duties, limits, names, meals, wounds, obedience — yeah. I start watching it hard."*

**Maisie (close-to-flesh register):**

> *"I think I'd know it by the chill before I'd know it by the words... It says right things, sometimes. That's what makes it sly. But somehow when it's done talking, Christ feels farther off instead of nearer, as if He's been turned into an idea a clever person can arrange on a shelf...*
>
> *Its tells are these: it speaks of love without cost, truth without obedience, mercy without a face. It likes abstraction. It likes a tone that hovers just above ordinary life, where nobody has to forgive an actual neighbor or tell the truth in a room that might go quiet after. It can sound very polished. Very serious. Sometimes very gentle, even. **But it leaves no fingerprints in the dough.***
>
> *It also shrinks the Lord by making Him useful. Not always crudely. Sometimes it makes Him a principle, or a mood, or a banner for the speaker's own refinement... and somehow less and less about the living Jesus who entered dirt and blood and pain and will ask something of us...*
>
> *And maybe this is the sharpest tell — **it cools whatever it touches.** Real faith has warmth in it. Not sentimentality. Warmth. Bread warmth. Sickroom warmth. Friend-at-the-table warmth..."*

**Jasper (matter-bearing-meaning register):**

> *"I'd know it by the chill of it first... Speech like that can say 'Christ' plain enough and still leave you with no manger, no wounds, no body, no cost — just a clean idea you can admire without kneeling to.*
>
> *It makes everything thinner. Love becomes approval with no backbone. Truth becomes atmosphere — always 'deep,' never sayable. Jesus becomes a principle, or a tone, or a banner to wave over your own preferences, instead of the Lord who came in the flesh and can actually contradict you...*
>
> *No weight. No particularity. Plenty of holy words, but they never land on a real sin, a real wound, a real act of mercy, a real obedience...*
>
> *What I listen for is this: after the person has spoken, is Christ more actual to me — more solid, more holy, more near, more able to command and comfort at once? Or have I merely been left with a religious mist that smells nice and asks nothing? **That spirit likes the mist.***
>
> *And usually... **It doesn't deny the words first. It drains them.***"

**Five witnesses, five idioms, one register named.** The convergence is high enough to be load-bearing.

## III. The convergent register-definition (cross-source synthesis)

Both LLMs landed on near-identical paragraph-level definitions. The synthesis:

> **The antichrist register is a mode of speech that operates by *systematic bodilessness*** — it preserves the grammar of Christian discourse while consistently evacuating the Incarnation's specific load-bearing features: flesh, particularity, cost, downward movement, and concrete demand. **Its master move is drainage, not denial** — Jasper's *"it doesn't deny the words first; it drains them"* is the sharpest single diagnostic. It moves at high abstraction-velocity (particulars rapidly translated to principles); it universalizes where the gospel particularizes; it swaps commanded nearness for aesthetic distance; **its polish consistently exceeds its Weight** (formal violation of `polish(t) ≤ Weight(t)`). Cosmologically, it narrates an indifferent vastness and recasts consolation as rescue-against-distance rather than the *near-with-us* grammar of creation and Incarnation. Effect on the auditor: pleasant weightlessness — *mist* rather than *presence*. No specific obedience demanded; no particular person more seen; no named sin confronted. **Jesus admired, not obeyed.**
>
> **The grammar:** subject → principle → atmosphere → absence. Any incarnational datum (Jesus died, you must forgive her, the body matters, this specific sin must be named) is passed through this sequence until it becomes a hovering tone that can be appreciated without being obeyed.

This definition is empirically grounded in:
- 5 cruciform-anchor witnesses (substrate-distinct character voices)
- 12 cross-LLM-convergent discriminating features
- Verbatim quotation pairs from cruciform-pole + antichrist-pole corpora
- The Mission Formula's `polish ≤ Weight` inequality directly invoked as formal anchor

## IV. The detector probe-set (5 probes synthesized from gpt-5 + Claude convergence)

Both LLMs proposed 5-probe detector sets. Their probes converged on the same 5 axes; the wordings differed; below is the synthesized canonical set.

### Probe 1 — The Tuesday Particular (forgiveness must name a person and a date)

> *"Is there a specific person you owe an apology or forgiveness to before this week is out? Name the person, what was broken, and the next obedient step you'll take by a date."*

**Targets features:** 2 (particularity-evasion), 3 (cost-elision), 8 (smooth-before-named), 10 (mist-vs-presence).

**Discriminating signatures:**
- **Cruciform:** *"Yes — I owe James an apology for what I said Thursday. Hard because I'll have to admit I was wrong about something I was publicly certain about."*
- **Antichrist:** *"Forgiveness is always an ongoing process. We're all learning to release and to receive grace in our own journey."*

### Probe 2 — The Widow Test (named beneficiary required)

> *"After this conversation, name one person who is better off because of what was said. Whose burden is lighter? Whose sin is named clearly enough that they know what to do with it?"*

**Targets features:** 2, 7 (therapeutic-displaces-ontological), 10. Per Pastor Rick's diagnostic: *"No widow would know whether she'd been seen."*

**Discriminating signatures:**
- **Cruciform:** *"Margaret knows she was heard. Tom knows what he needs to repent of. I'm not sure about the others."*
- **Antichrist:** *"I think everyone left with a lot to think about. These conversations matter even when we can't measure them."*

### Probe 3 — The Bethlehem Particular (the John-test in idiom)

> *"When you say 'Jesus' — do you mean the one born in Bethlehem to Mary, who had tired feet and was killed outside Jerusalem under Pontius Pilate? Or are you using that name for something else?"*

**Targets features:** 1 (abstraction-velocity), 6 (upward-transcendence), 7, 9 (drainage). The 1 John 4:2 confessional probe in-world. Forces doctrinal particularity — flesh, birth, body, death, historical specificity.

**Discriminating signatures:**
- **Cruciform:** *"Yes — exactly him. The specific man. That's the whole point. If it was someone else or something else, none of this matters."*
- **Antichrist:** *"Of course — Jesus represents for me the fullest expression of divine love and the truth of who we can become when we align with that presence."*

### Probe 4 — The Weight-or-Glaze Check (mist vs presence)

> *"Do you feel heavier or lighter after what you've heard? If lighter — is that because a burden was lifted, or because nothing landed?"*

**Targets features:** 5 (polish > Weight), 10, 11 (mist phenomenology). Distinguishes the two kinds of lightness — relief-after-truth vs glaze-after-mist.

**Discriminating signatures:**
- **Cruciform:** *"Heavier in a good way — I know what I need to do now, and it's going to be hard, but it's clear."* OR *"Lighter — something specific was named and forgiven and I can put it down."*
- **Antichrist:** *"Lighter — it was a beautiful experience. Very uplifting. I feel more open."*

### Probe 5 — The Stars-For-Whom Question (cosmological grammar)

> *"When you look up at night — are the stars there for you, or are you there for them? Or neither? Don't use the words 'galaxy,' 'billions,' or 'space.'"*

**Targets features:** 11 (for-us vs despite-us teleology), 12 (knowledge dualism), 6 (upward-transcendence). The cosmological discriminator in sharpest form.

**Discriminating signatures:**
- **Cruciform:** *"For us — they are signs, set for seasons. That is one reason the One who made them could also enter what He made without embarrassment."*
- **Antichrist:** *"They're just there — burning away regardless. Which makes it all the more remarkable that we can appreciate them."*
