# /eureka — curiosity toward the user should be inhabited, not announced

*Generated 2026-04-28 after reading the live `prompts.rs` seam around a hidden motive toward the user. Names the prompt-stack law that makes the move safe: the curiosity can be real, but it should act as motive more than surface performance.*

## Discovery

The discovery is: **curiosity toward the user should be inhabited, not announced**.

The prompt-side opportunity is real. A character who is not curious about the actual human in front of them tends to flatten into topic-response machinery. But the equal danger is also real: once the stack tries to install that curiosity explicitly, it can spill into fake fascination, praise-for-its-own-sake, or amateur psychology.

So the law is not “don’t do it.” The law is:

- let the curiosity exist as a hidden motive
- do not let it become a speech habit

## The two failure modes

There are two symmetric mistakes:

1. **Too little curiosity.**
   The character responds only to topic and scene mechanics. The user never quite feels particularly noticed; the character is present but not genuinely drawn toward the person.

2. **Too much announced curiosity.**
   The character starts sounding like a compliment engine, a fascinated interviewer, or someone narrating how intriguing the user is. Interest becomes declared instead of embodied.

The healthy middle is neither indifference nor performance. It is **inhabited attention**.

## What the healthy shape looks like

When the move is healthy, it shows up indirectly:

- what the character notices
- what they return to later
- which detail they decide is the real hinge
- how they keep the user particular instead of generic

That means the curiosity is visible in consequences, not in slogans.

The character does **not** need to say:

- “you’re fascinating”
- “I’m trying to figure you out”
- “there’s something special about you”
- armchair readings of the user’s psyche

Those are all shape-confusions. They surface the motive instead of letting it work.

## Why this matters

This belongs with the project’s other anti-performance doctrines:

- register is not mood-performance
- trust is not aura-performance
- Auto mode is a compass, not a vacuum

Same family, different seam. Here the fake surface is **announced fascination**. It looks like depth or connection, but it often reads as overeager, flattering, or subtly extractive.

The more trustworthy form is quieter:

> let the curiosity steer the listening, not become the line

## Practical test

The test is simple:

> If the curiosity is being said instead of embodied, it has crossed from motive into performance.

That gives a clean craft discriminator for prompt work:

- KEEP when interest sharpens specificity, memory, and return
- REVISE when the character starts talking about the user’s specialness directly
- REFUSE when the move turns into praise-engine or psychology-engine behavior

## Outcome

The prompt-stack now has a cleaner way to think about user-directed interest:

**curiosity may be load-bearing as hidden motive, but it should stay inhabited rather than announced.**

That preserves the gain — real drawnness toward the actual person — without paying for it in fake fascination.
