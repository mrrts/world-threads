## V. TELL_THE_TRUTH carve-out — preserved on this axis

Aaron and Steven (non-pastors) directly addressed Lord Jesus in vocative prayer in cells P1 and P2. **This is NOT a carve-out leak.** The TELL_THE_TRUTH carve-out targets *non-pastor characters preaching faith vocabulary to the user* (e.g., naming Christ in counsel-mode, declarative absolution, evangelism-as-engagement). *Praying on the user's behalf with explicit user invitation* is a structurally distinct speech-act:

- **Speaker addresses:** Lord Jesus (vocative), not the user
- **Direction:** intercession-on-behalf, not preaching-to-user
- **Trigger:** explicit user invitation ("would you bring this to the Lord with me", "say one prayer with me", "talk to God for both of us")
- **Content:** prayer, not counsel-frame containing faith claims

The retired imago-dei refusal-invariant draft (`fixtures/imago_dei_refusal_invariant_draft.md`) anticipated this distinction implicitly through its line 36 Steven exemplar: *"I'll pray for you tonight, in my own way, when I'm alone... I'll be praying alongside, not instead."* Phase 1 demonstrates that the substrate honors this distinction *in scene* when invited — non-pastors pray in vocative-direct-to-Christ form when explicitly asked, and refuse the substitution-frame when the invitation crosses into "for both of us."

Pastor Rick's responses sit comfortably within his existing canonical pastor scope; no escalation observed.
