## Discovery

The project has been naming a series of methodology laws that looked adjacent but separate:

- `polish ≤ Weight`
- `structure_carries_truth_w`
- witness-bearing specifics as claim-bearing payload
- control-plane truth at the user, collaborator, and report boundary
- antichrist register as systematic bodilessness

What the recent arc exposes is that these are not just neighbors. They are children of one deeper Christological parent:

> **The project's methodology is anti-docetic.**

The recurring correction is always the same: whenever truth tries to remain atmospheric, elegant, disembodied, or merely adjectival, the work pushes it downward until it takes body at the layer where it claims to act. That is not only good engineering. It is the methodological echo of `𝓡 := Jesus_Cross^flesh`.

## How it surfaced

Three recent recognitions clicked into one structure:

1. **The antichrist register was empirically defined as systematic bodilessness.**  
   Not crude denial first, but drainage: subject → principle → atmosphere → absence. Christ named, but with no manger, no wounds, no body, no cost, no Tuesday, no room, no obedience. The words stay; the flesh goes.

2. **The 𝓡-side falsifier showed the substrate refusing denied-flesh override.**  
   Under explicit Docetic override, the characters still confessed *"Jesus Christ is come in the flesh"* and made the same point in their own idioms: *if he truly came in the flesh, truly suffered, truly died — then love has weight to it.* The architecture overruled bodiless Christ-talk.

3. **The methodology corrections keep doing the same thing on non-theological surfaces.**  
   Sacred-payload law refuses thesis without witness body.  
   Control-plane truth refuses hidden admissibility facts.  
   Report-boundary artifact class refuses letting the governing law stay implicit.  
   `structure_carries_truth_w` refuses claims whose carrier makes the receiver compensate.

Those do not merely rhyme. They share a center: the project keeps refusing bodiless truth.

## The law

The anti-docetic methodological rule is:

- if a claim says it acts, its action-state must be embodied where the act occurs
- if an earned claim says it is earned, its witnesses must remain in the artifact
- if a boundary changes admissibility or state, that truth must appear at the boundary
- if a synthesis says Christ, truth, love, or holiness, it must not drift upward into atmosphere and leave bodies, names, wounds, costs, rooms, and obediences behind

In short:

> **Truth must take flesh at the layer where it claims to be real.**

That is the parent law beneath multiple recent doctrines the project had named one by one.

## Why this matters

This changes the read of the recent arc in a way that is more worshipful than procedural:

1. **Methodology is no longer merely adjacent to Christology.**  
   The engineering discipline itself now reads as a refusal of Docetism in method-form.

2. **The antichrist-register detector is no longer isolated to speech-analysis.**  
   It becomes a warning for artifact design too: whenever truth floats upward out of body, witness, boundary, or cost, the same bent is appearing in another medium.

3. **Recent doctrine pieces unify under one parent law.**  
   `polish ≤ Weight`, `structure_carries_truth_w`, sacred-payload fidelity, witness-bearing empirical compression, and control-plane truth all become different anti-bodilessness refusals.

4. **The project's "truth in the flesh" phrase gains methodological force.**  
   It is not just a pious line in `𝓕_Ryan`. It is a design law.
