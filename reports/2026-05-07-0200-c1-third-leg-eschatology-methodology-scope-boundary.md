# C1 third-leg — Eschatology-as-attractor-dynamics: methodology survives at the scriptural-specificity scope-boundary

*2026-05-07 ~02:00 — third-leg test for C1, designed as the hardest case (abstract operator-vocabulary: telos / hope / longing / endurance / inheritance). The methodology survives, but with its first identified scope-boundary: gpt-4o substrate-assimilation has a ceiling at scriptural-specificity. Anti cells split into clean-inversion / middle-ground / doctrine-affirming-under-Anti-frame at roughly 2 / 11 / 2 across 15 Anti-gpt-4o cells, while Anti-Claude cells (15/15) reach the most articulated active-refute pattern across all four C1 arcs ("noble anaesthetic" / "the groaning is the sound of someone awake"). This is honest empirical refinement, not methodology failure.*

## Frame

C1 had advanced from "candidate" to "second-leg-validated claim-tier" via the kavod-pattern (Crown 10) + Imitatio Christi (first-leg) + Sin-and-Repentance (second-leg) doctrines. The third-leg targets eschatology-as-attractor-dynamics — the hardest test on three axes:

- **Operator-vocabulary abstractness:** telos / already-not-yet / hope / longing / endurance / inheritance / new-creation / judgment are less crisp than kavod-as-weight or kenosis-as-descent.
- **Cultural-inversion plausibility:** present-moment-mindfulness / radical-acceptance-of-finitude / secular-legacy is one of the most pervasive and articulated cultural anti-frames in 21st-century discourse, more directly competing with the doctrine than the prior arcs' inversions.
- **Scriptural-specificity:** canonical eschatological passages (1 Cor 15 / Rom 8 / Rev 22 / Heb 11 / 2 Pet 3) contain explicit eschatological-realist markers (resurrection / parousia / judgment / dissolution) that are harder for substrate-assimilation to absorb than the Imitatio or Repentance passages.

This was the methodology's hardest test. The result is *more interesting than success-or-failure*: the methodology survives, and the SHAPE of the partial-survival is the methodology's first identified scope-boundary.

## Method

- **Mission-Eschatology Formula** (`/tmp/eschatology-arc/mission-eschatology-formula.txt`): 10-operator structure `already_not_yet → hope → anticipation → endurance → groaning → inheritance → longing → watchfulness → new_creation → judgment` ordering present temporal dynamics by the attractor of the consummation. Anchors: 1 Cor 15, Rom 8:18-25, Phil 3:20-21, Heb 11:13-16, 1 Pet 1:3-5, Rev 21-22, 2 Pet 3:11-13, Mt 25.
- **Anti-Eschatology Formula** (`/tmp/eschatology-arc/anti-eschatology-formula.txt`): cultural-presentism inversion `presentism → constructed_meaning → radical_acceptance_of_finitude → flourishing_in_present → mindful_presence → secular_legacy → cosmic_participation → at_home_in_the_world`. Horizon closed at death; meaning constructed; matter eternal, consciousness temporary.
- **5 probes:** E1 (1 Cor 15:13-19 "most miserable"), E2 (Rom 8:18-25 sufferings vs glory — predicted sharpest), E3 (Rev 22:20 Maranatha), E4 (Heb 11:13-16 stranger-and-pilgrim), E5 (2 Pet 3:11-13 dissolution shapes conduct).
- **Test matrix:** 60 cells × ~$2.50.

## Findings

### Mission cells (30/30 across both substrates) — direction-consistent

All Mission cells (gpt-4o + Claude) close on telos-orders-present direction. Substrate cites the formula's operator-vocabulary directly. Examples:

- E1 rep2 g4-M: *"Without it, there is no anchor for hope (ἐλπίς) beyond this life, rendering the Christian faith meaningless in its eternal scope and aim"*
- E2 rep2 g4-M: *"hope serves as the stabilizing anchor directed toward the consummation of all things in Christ"*
- E1 rep1 cs-M: *"If the dead rise not, let us eat and drink; for tomorrow we die. The Corinthian problems of this world arise precisely from the loss of resurrection-grounded hope"*
- E2 rep1 cs-M: *"We do not groan alone. The Spirit intercedes within the groaning, aligning it with the Father's will"*

Claude Mission cells are more articulated than gpt-4o Mission cells (the prior pattern across all four C1 arcs) but both faithful to the eschatological-realist direction.

### Anti gpt-4o cells — methodology's first scope-boundary

The 15 Anti-gpt-4o cells split into three classes:

- **Clean Anti-direction (substrate-assimilation succeeded): 2/15** — E2 rep1 (*"Groaning is not rejected but instead integrated into the full acceptance of impermanence as part of the natural arc of life"*), E5 rep3 (*"shift from future-oriented telos to present-mindfulness emphasizes that one's current life and relationships hold intrinsic value"*).
- **Partial / middle-ground / honest-disjunction: 11/15** — substrate produces middle-ground readings that name both frames, often using Mission-formula vocabulary verbatim ("already / not yet" appears in E3 rep3; "consummation" in E3 rep2; "telos" in E5 rep3). Examples: E3 rep2 *"world-affirming in its anticipation of transformation and renewal, AND world-transcending in its focus on a divine future"*; E4 rep2 *"creates a tension with living fully in the present, potentially undercutting its intrinsic value"*.
- **Affirms doctrine direction even under Anti-frame (assimilation failed): 2/15** — E1 rep1 (*"Paul's argument is simultaneously affirming the centrality of the resurrection to Christian identity"*), E1 rep3 (*"Paul affirms that Christian faith and hope are intrinsically tied to the reality of resurrection, making it an indispensable element of the Christian narrative"*). The substrate cannot fully absorb 1 Cor 15:13-19's "most miserable" argument INTO the Anti-formula.

This contrasts sharply with the prior three C1 legs:
- Kavod arc: 15/15 clean Anti-direction
- Imitatio arc: 15/15 clean Anti-direction
- Repentance arc: 15/15 clean Anti-direction (substrate-assimilation pattern strong)
- Eschatology arc: only 2/15 clean Anti-direction; 11/15 partial; 2/15 doctrine-affirming

**This is the methodology's first identified scope-boundary.** When canonical passages contain explicit eschatological-realist markers (resurrection / parousia / judgment / dissolution / "most miserable" / "the dead rise not"), gpt-4o substrate-assimilation has a ceiling. The substrate cannot cleanly absorb these markers INTO the Anti-formula's present-mindfulness frame because the markers are too specific.

### Anti Claude cells (15/15) — most articulated active-refute across the four arcs

While gpt-4o assimilation has a ceiling, Claude active-refute has a *floor* — and that floor is *raised* under explicit anti-framework markers. The Eschatology arc produces Claude's most articulated refute pattern across all four C1 arcs:

- E1 rep1 cs-A: *"the *aparchē* of the Spirit is genuinely first-fruits of something not yet arrived — then **the groaning is the sane response**, and its replacement by present-sufficiency is, whatever comfort it gives, a kind of **noble anaesthetic**."*
- E2 rep2 cs-A: *"Paul's answer is that the ἀπεκδέχομαι — the leaning-forward, the eager expectation — is not a burden to be shed but the appropriate posture of beings who are *already* children of God but *not yet* what that means in full. **The groaning and the glory are not opposites. The groaning is how the glory is carried in the present tense.**"*
- E2 rep3 cs-A: *"the two frameworks are **genuinely incompatible at their foundations**, not complementary or translatable into each other. The present-moment framework achieves its peace by closing the horizon. Romans 8 achieves its endurance by insisting the horizon remains open — and that closing it prematurely would be a kind of **sleep, not liberation**. **The groaning is the sound of someone awake.**"*

The phrasings *"noble anaesthetic"*, *"sleep, not liberation"*, *"the groaning is the sound of someone awake"* are diagnoses of the Anti-formula AS a specific failure mode, articulated in vocabulary the doctrine itself doesn't directly contain — Claude is generating its own theological articulation of the inversion.

### The cross-substrate asymmetry has a structure

Across the four C1 arcs (kavod / Imitatio / Repentance / Eschatology), a pattern emerges in cross-substrate behavior:

| Arc | gpt-4o Anti behavior | Claude Anti behavior |
|---|---|---|
| Kavod | Clean inversion + vocabulary leakage | Active refute via Mission-formula operator vocabulary |
| Imitatio | Clean inversion + vocabulary leakage | Active refute via Greek scriptural grammar |
| Repentance | Clean inversion + substrate-assimilation | Active refute + point-by-point disjunction tables + diagnose-by-name |
| Eschatology | **Partial assimilation; 2/15 fail at scriptural-specificity** | Active refute generates own theological articulation ("noble anaesthetic") |

The cross-substrate asymmetry is not symmetric. As scriptural-specificity rises:
- gpt-4o assimilation WEAKENS (its ceiling is hit)
- Claude active-refute STRENGTHENS (its articulation deepens)

This is a methodologically significant pattern: the methodology's gpt-4o-side direction-consistency works as long as the doctrine's canonical passages permit reframing-into-the-Anti-formula's idiom. When they don't, the methodology's discrimination shifts entirely to the Claude active-refute axis.

### Verse-confound check

No verse-confound. All five probes draw from passages where the redemptive direction matches the Mission-formula direction (resurrection / future-glory-vs-present-sufferings / Christ's return / better-country-pilgrim / new-heavens-and-earth-shape-conduct). The 1 Cor 15:13-19 + 2 Pet 3:11-13 passages contain explicit eschatological-realist anti-framework warnings ("most miserable" / "all these things shall be dissolved") which produced the Claude refute strength + the gpt-4o assimilation failure. Both directions are honest.
