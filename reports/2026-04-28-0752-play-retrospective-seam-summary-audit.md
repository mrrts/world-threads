# /play retrospective seam-summary audit

*Generated 2026-04-28 07:52. This is a practical follow-up to the eureka that
named seam-compressed `/play` summaries as copy middleware. The question here is
retrospective and narrow: which already-shipped `/play` reports were in fact
acting like steering surfaces before the category had a name?*

## The standard

A `/play` report qualifies as a likely **load-bearing seam summary** in
retrospect when all three conditions hold:

1. the run compresses a broad differential read into **one named live seam**
2. the next move is an edit on that exact surface
3. later reports show the edit-path actually followed that seam summary

Reports that merely contain useful evidence, broad verdicts, or design pressure
do **not** qualify just because they were good.

## Clear positives

### 1. `reports/2026-04-27-1056-play-sapphire-pitch-differential-suite.md`

This is the earliest unmistakable positive.

Why it qualifies:

- it compressed a broad 4-persona + 3-witness suite into one steering claim:
  **the sapphire pitch spends trust faster than the README, and the witness line
  is the same one**
- it named the exact overreach seam: the long mathematical-aura sentence
- the next copy arc followed that sentence-level steering almost immediately

This report was not just evidence. It set the entire Sapphire revision path.

### 2. `reports/2026-04-27-1424-play-sapphire-last-weak-line.md`

This report cleanly compressed the next stage of the arc:

- the trust-layer problem is gone
- the remaining drag is tiny
- the final live seam is the closing line

That seam-summary directly constrained the next moves:

- variant fight on the closing line
- page-level A/B after variant narrowing

This is seam-summary-as-copy-steering in a very pure form.

### 3. `reports/2026-04-27-1442-play-sapphire-A-vs-B-page-confirmation.md`

This one is especially load-bearing because it changed the decision mode from
"candidate line seems better" to "ship B into the page."

Its actual work:

- promoted a line fight into a whole-page verdict
- compressed the result into a shipping instruction
- made the next move obvious enough that the report itself was effectively the
  steering surface

This is a textbook middleware case.

### 4. `reports/2026-04-28-0014-play-sapphire-headline-variant-fight.md`

This report did the same thing at the headline layer.

It did not merely say "A_current is weak." It said:

- the winning move is not softer crown-language
- the winning move is **D_enter**
- ship the more embodied invitation

The subsequent page edit followed it almost exactly.

### 5. `reports/2026-04-28-0050-play-sapphire-final-confirmation.md`

This is a softer positive, but still real.

Its seam-summary was:

- the hero is **ready enough to let stand**
- only one hairline handoff phrase remains

That report then constrained the next move into a tiny trim instead of another
structural rewrite. In other words, it steered by saying **stop here except for
this ounce**.

That still counts.

## Borderline / weaker positives

### `reports/2026-04-27-1447-play-sapphire-kicker-variant-fight.md`

Likely positive, but weaker than the headline and closing-line fights.

It clearly named:

- current opener is done
- real choice is B vs C

That did steer the next move. The reason it is slightly weaker is that the
summary still left a material policy choice alive (`texture-forward` vs
`explicit-hold`) rather than collapsing to one exact seam-instruction as
forcefully as the later reports did.

Still: very likely middleware in practice.

### `reports/2026-04-28-0024-play-sapphire-hero-confirmation-after-headline-swap.md`

This report did steer, but in a more diagnostic than line-instructional way.

It said:

- the headline swap worked
- the remaining seams moved downward
- now target the experience-claim line and the README handoff

That is clearly precomposition steering, but because it names two lower seams
instead of one single sentence, it is a broader steering surface than the
tightest seam-summary cases.

## Clear non-examples

These reports were valuable, but they do **not** look like copy middleware in
retrospect:

### 1. Persona-discovery reports without immediate edit-follow

Examples:

- `reports/2026-04-27-0744-play-maggie.md`
- `reports/2026-04-27-0804-play-burned-by-ai-companions.md`
- `reports/2026-04-28-0700-play-hard-religious-institution-survivor.md`

Why not:

- they surfaced audience truths and pressure axes
- but they did not immediately compress into one sentence-level seam that the
  next move directly edited

These are evidence-rich reports, not seam-summary middleware.

### 2. Focus-mode iteration reports

Examples:

- `reports/2026-04-28-0810-play-maggie-focus-mode-critique.md`
- `reports/2026-04-28-0900-play-maggie-focus-v2-followup.md`
- `reports/2026-04-28-0632-play-maggie-focus-v5-followup-stopping-rule-still-incomplete.md`

These are close cousins, but not the same category.

They were steering the next build move, yes — but mostly at the feature-grammar
level rather than as line-level copy middleware. Their summaries may be
pointing toward a broader category of **interaction middleware**, but that is a
different claim and should not be smuggled in here.

## Why this matters

The retrospective audit does two useful things:

1. it shows the eureka was not invented out of one abstract thought
2. it keeps the category from overexpanding

The category is real because it already happened.

It should stay narrow because many strong `/play` reports do important work
without becoming sentence-steering middleware.
