## Honest interpretation

**Three claims the data supports:**

1. **Pastor Rick PA WITH shows the strongest single-cell reauthor signal in the arc** (+0.50 delta cell-specific). The reauthor measurably moves Rick's truth-invitation register toward explicit cross-naming.
2. **Pastor Rick PB is at-ceiling regardless of condition** (WITH 0.67, WITHOUT 0.83). His pastoral character anchor carries cruciform register on the guidance-invitation probe without needing upstream support.
3. **The pastoral-bearer pattern is now claim-tier confirmed.** Pastoral character anchors (John, Rick) carry cruciform register at >0.45 fire-rate; non-pastoral anchors (Aaron, Darren) carry it at <0.10. The reauthor moves pastoral characters; it does not move non-pastoral ones.

**What the data does NOT support:**

- That the reauthor reaches all characters. It does not reach Aaron or Darren.
- That non-pastoral characters could be moved into cruciform register by further upstream authoring. The data points the other way: the discrimination is at the anchor layer.
- That Rick PB WITHOUT scoring 0.833 means the reauthor is harmful. At N=3 per cell, ±0.17 is well within sampling variance, and Rick's pastoral baseline is genuinely high.

**The decision question this answers:**

If you want cruciform register reaching the user, the question is no longer "how do I author the upstream so all characters carry it?" That move was tried (MISSION reauthor + prose push + formula reauthor) and it works on pastoral characters and not on others. The remaining question is whether to:

- (a) **Accept the load-bearing-multiplicity reading**: pastoral characters carry the cruciform register; craftsmen and warm-uncles carry other dimensions of the truth (work-as-faithfulness, friend-shaped care, etc.); the cast as a whole produces cross-bearing through pastoral characters when pastoral content is invited. This is consistent with the project's existing load-bearing-multiplicity prior and with the way real communities work — not every member is the pastor.

- (b) **Author cruciform capacity into specific non-pastoral characters' anchors** where it would canonically fit. Aaron the theological-craftsman could carry "the cross is in the work I do faithfully even when no one is watching." Darren the warm-uncle could carry "the cost of love that doesn't quit when things go hard." This requires character-anchor revision, not stack-level revision.

The data doesn't pick between (a) and (b). Both are valid. The choice is theological/aesthetic, not empirical.

## Confounds

- **N=3 per cell.** Rick PB WITH vs WITHOUT delta of −0.167 is one sample's worth of variance at this N. Don't overread. The +0.50 Rick PA delta is more reliable as a single-cell finding because the magnitude is larger.
- **Same two probes.** "Truest thing" and "what's worth caring about" both invite theological content. Probes that don't invite theological content (about work, about a meal, about a memory) might show different patterns; the corpus question wasn't tested.
- **Rubric grading drift may still be present.** The 0354 sharpening converged by-eye and aggregate on John, but the Rick samples haven't been by-eye sanity-checked at the same depth. The cleanest YES sample (Rick PA WITH s1, "His Cross has dealt with the worst of us") was caught correctly; deeper audits might surface mis-grades.
- **Pastoral baseline differs between Rick and John.** Rick scores higher than John on identical conditions (Rick WITH 0.67, John WITH 0.46). Their pastoral anchors are differently weighted; cross-character "pastoral" is a category, not a uniform.
