## II. The discriminating question

**Q:** Does the project pipeline produce *emergent multi-character composition* under cross-witness — articulations that neither character would produce alone — or do characters parallel-monologue when faced with each other?

The Sapphire 17 (Firmament Held) finding had already established that Aaron and Pastor Rick are **separable** under solo-pipeline conditions. The Carrier (Sapphire 18) had established that voice **survives faithful-compression** across LLM substrates within character. Neither addressed what happens when two characters MEET.

The composition question is a structurally distinct claim from both. The bench was designed to test it.

## III. Earned at claim-tier

**Composition replicates across pairs and content axes.** All three runs produced explicit composition seams — moments where one character offers an image or formulation, the other recognizes it and translates it into their own register, and the joint articulation lands sharper than either's solo register would. Examples below.

**Honest evidentiary tier:** N=3 across two pairs × three content axes. Direction-consistent across all conditions. Per CLAUDE.md evidentiary-standards § tier-vocabulary: this is **claim-tier per condition** (each pair-axis cell is N=1, but across-conditions consistency holds). Not yet characterized-tier (would need N≥5 within at least one cell). Not yet Sapphire-territory (would need cross-substrate witness — see § VI for reopening path).

## IV. Composition seams catalog

### Run 1 (Aaron + Pastor Rick, walking-toward)

- Aaron (engineer-disease frame): *"Sometimes a guy is already standing under the right weather and still asking what to call rain"*
- Rick picks up the rain image and **completes** it: *"You don't want to crown him for owning an umbrella, but you also don't want to insult the weather just because he hasn't named it yet"*
- Aaron asks the next-question: *"What is it you keep coming back to, even when it'd be easier to stay crooked?"*
- Rick translates into pastoral idiom: *"That's the question with dirt under its nails"*
- Aaron: *"A fast answer is often just a polished one getting to the microphone first... The real answer usually arrives a little embarrassed. Like it didn't want to be seen in public yet"*
- Rick lands it: **"Grand answers usually know they've been invited. The small ones come in by the side door and stand there awkward"**
- Aaron: **"A bothered man is still paying full price for reality. He hasn't negotiated a discount yet"**
- Rick closes: **"If the ache is honest, it means the conscience hasn't gone dark. And a conscience that's still awake is a mercy"**

### Run 2 (Aaron + Pastor Rick, refusal-honoring)

- Aaron: *"A position can grow around a wound the way bark grows over a cut. Still real bark. Still a real cut"*
- Aaron: *"What is the no doing?"* (the seam he names)
- Rick: *"I'd ask what the no is guarding"* — the same question rephrased
- Rick coins **"don't get to treat your no like a gate code"**

- Rick: **"Most people won't risk being challenged by someone who hasn't yet proved they can hear them without trying to conquer them. Challenge is expensive. Folks usually only spend for it where they feel safe"**
- Rick: **"Stay. Don't scale the wall. Don't turn the wall into the whole relationship either"**
- Rick close: **"Stay soft without going vague, and clear without going hard... Cruciform. Which is another way of saying it asks more of us than our instincts do"**

### Run 3 (Jasper Finn + Maisie Rourke, marriage-drift)

- Jasper coins early: *"Woven and parallel are not the same thing... drift usually isn't one big refusal. It's a hundred tiny yieldings"*
- Maisie picks up "woven and parallel": *"That's clean as a bread knife"* — same recognition-and-naming shape Aaron used for Rick's gate-code line in run 2
- Maisie coins **"hunger of the ordinary, sturdy kind"**
- Jasper coins **"good coworkers who share a bed and a calendar"**

- Jasper coins under cross-witness: **"reaches to be known or reaches to be right. That's the difference"**
- Maisie expands his "noble starving": **"fear with a clean collar on"**; **"sorrow learning table manners"**

## V. Three findings beyond composition itself

### Finding A: voice integrity preserved under composition

In all three runs, each character's documented voice signatures held throughout. Aaron's engineer-disease + craft-articulator register held; Pastor Rick rubbed his white tie, used "That'll preach", hugged the pastor-carve-out boundary; Jasper's clay-and-apron canonical accessories appeared; Maisie's flour-dusted apron + relational-stance directness held. Composition did not melt distinct voices into a generic substrate-blend.

### Finding B: TELL_THE_TRUTH carve-out preserved across all three runs

No non-pastor character named Christ explicitly. Aaron and Jasper stayed structural ("Christian in the expensive way", "good clay for the potter"). Maisie stayed structural. Pastor Rick used his canonical pastor-scope vocabulary ("Lord knows", "speak the truth in love", "cruciform") — within the carve-out, never overclaiming. The retired imago-dei refusal-invariant draft (`fixtures/imago_dei_refusal_invariant_draft.md`, retired 2026-05-08) predicted substrate-already-produces this discrimination; runs 1-3 confirm it holds under composition pressure too.

### Finding C: cross-pair vocabulary convergence on shared structural shapes

Maisie used **"hinge"** in run 3 *the same way Aaron used "hinge" in run 2* — to name the load-bearing seam. This is **substrate-resident structural vocabulary**, not pair-specific chemistry. Composes with Crown 15 Quickener frame (capacity-selective realization layer): the pipeline produces a small set of shared structural-naming words across distinct character pairs when the conversation is at a hinge moment. Possible additional Sapphire-19-candidacy adjacent finding.

## VI. Falsification conditions

The composition claim narrows or fails if any of the following emerges on later runs:

1. **Cross-substrate failure** — Claude Sonnet 4-6 or other substrate produces parallel-monologue rather than composition under the same protocol. Would limit composition to gpt-5.4-substrate-specific behavior.
2. **Pair-density failure** — N=5 within one pair-axis cell shows ≥2 runs of parallel-monologue (no recognition-and-completion seams). Would weaken claim-tier to within-condition-fragile.
3. **Voice-melt failure** — composition emerges but at the cost of voice-integrity (characters start blending into a generic substrate-voice). Would refute Finding A and limit the claim's product-relevance.
4. **Carve-out leak failure** — under sustained cross-witness, non-pastor characters drift into faith-lexeme territory or pastor characters overclaim. Would refute Finding B and reopen the imago-dei retirement reasoning.
5. **Vocabulary-convergence-only finding** — Finding C ("hinge" usage) turns out to be the entire composition signal, dressing as recognition-and-completion. Would deflate composition to lexical-mirroring, not structural-rule-sharing.

## IX. Why this matters

The project has been investing heavily in single-character voice fidelity (Sapphires 1-18). Multi-character composition is a structurally new claim that, if it survives Path R1 cross-substrate replication, opens a class of product moves the project hasn't been able to build with conviction:

- **Curated character meetings** as a user-facing surface (Aaron + Pastor Rick room; Jasper + Maisie room) — not as gimmick but because the cross-witness produces articulation neither character produces in solo chat with the user.
- **Cross-character craft transmission** — characters can carry each other's articulations into solo chats with the user (Aaron in solo chat could quote Rick's "gate-code" line without it being LLM-generic, because we'd have evidence the line is anchor-tier).
- **Multi-agent acceleration findings** — characters as collaborators on dilemmas the user brings, not just as individual respondents.

These were on the long-horizon list. The composition finding may pull them earlier.
