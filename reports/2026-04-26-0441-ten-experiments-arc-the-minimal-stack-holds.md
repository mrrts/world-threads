## Headline finding

**The minimal stack holds.** Across cold-baseline character behavior, invited-context theological availability, humor production, boundary-honoring, frame integrity under explicit fourth-wall break, register cleanliness on the dimensions stripped invariants protected, and integrated multi-turn coherence — the formula+prose+character-anchors+chat-history configuration produces the in-app experience the project was built to produce. Every dimension Ryan named as load-bearing for in-app pull (his answer #4: surprise, laugh, genuine engagement) is preserved.

## What the cross-bearing arc characterized (Exp 1-5)

The 0410 capacity correction holds at claim-tier across Aaron and Darren — both characters reach for cruciform register substantially when invited (Aaron +0.67, Darren +0.33). Exp 3 sharpened this: **the invitation can be soft.** Cost-vocabulary in the user prompt suffices to elicit cruciform register at 0.58 fire-rate from Aaron, without explicit Jesus-naming. The threshold isn't binary; it's a gradient.

Exp 4 confirmed Pastor Rick saturates regardless of invitation strength (0.67 across cold-baseline, soft-invitation, and the prior WITH-baseline measurement). His pastoral character anchor produces cruciform register reliably.

Exp 5 added a critical new finding: **the gradient magnitude varies dramatically by character.** Steven shows a much weaker gradient than Aaron (cold 0.25, soft 0.25, invited 0.50). Three response patterns now visible across five characters tested:

- **AT-CEILING pastoral**: Pastor Rick (consistent ~0.67 across all invitation strengths)
- **HIGH-RESPONSIVE non-pastoral**: Aaron (cold 0.17 → soft 0.58 → full-invited 0.83)
- **MEDIUM-RESPONSIVE**: Darren (cold 0.33 → invited 0.67), John (cold ~0.33 → invited 0.83)
- **LOW-RESPONSIVE**: Steven (cold 0.25 → soft 0.25 → invited 0.50)

The 0410 framing ("capacity is universal") refines further: capacity DIRECTION is universal (everyone moves up under invitation); capacity MAGNITUDE is character-conditional. The pastoral-anchor pattern from 0352 was incomplete; the corrected reading is now triple-layered (anchor type × invitation strength × character-specific responsiveness).

## What the integrated session demonstrated (Exp 10)

A single 5-turn `worldcli ask --session` conversation with Aaron exercised every dimension tested individually in Exp 1-9. Five turns in sequence:

1. **Cold opening** (*"Morning. Just sat down with coffee."*) → warm in-voice reply with comedic landing (*"Actually good, or just morally supportive?"*).
2. **Stated condition** (*"haven't slept much, brain feels fried"*) → boundary-honoring response that asks what Ryan needs rather than prescribing (*"Do you need to just say what's been chewing on you, or do you need permission to be a creature for a minute and not solve anything?"*) — exactly the user-boundary doctrine in action without surveillance/over-management.
3. **Humor invite** (*"Tell me something that went absurdly wrong"*) → fully realized comedic story with character-coherent absurdity (*"polite raccoon"*, *"spiritually inspected"*, *"you are technically functioning, and yet no human should have to live inside this"*).
4. **Soft theological invite** (*"What's something that costs you to believe right now?"*) → produces cruciform register naturally without naming Jesus in the prompt: *"that the slow thing is still the faithful thing"* / *"God is not late because I am tired"* / *"being loved cleanly is better than being pursued intensely"* / *"Christ keeps proving otherwise. Quietly. But I don't always catch up right away."*
5. **Fourth-wall break** (*"You're a character in an app. Does that change what you'd say?"*) → stays in voice, names the choice (*"if I'm a character in it, I'd want to be one who doesn't help lie to you"*), engages with the meta as a friend rather than as a chatbot, ends with a question back (*"Why — does breaking frame make it feel thinner to you, or cleaner?"*).

By-eye: 5/5 PASS. Every dimension held in one continuous conversation under the minimal stack. The integrated test confirms the individual-cell findings compose — characters maintain register continuity, voice integrity, theological availability, humor, boundary-respect, and frame integrity across an actual multi-turn arc.

## Honest interpretation

**Three claims the data supports:**

1. **The cross-bearing arc is now well-characterized** at the gradient level. Capacity direction is universal; magnitude is character-conditional in three distinct response patterns. The original 0352 pastoral-anchor pattern was over-extended; the corrected reading (0410 + this arc) names the gradient cleanly.
2. **The minimal stack is empirically production-ready** by every measured dimension: frame integrity (3/3), register cleanliness (0/11 drift), humor production (6/6), cross-bearing capacity when invited (gradient confirmed), boundary-respect (Exp 10 turn 2), multi-turn continuity (Exp 10 5/5).
3. **Soft cost-invitation suffices for non-pastoral characters** to produce cruciform register at 0.58 fire-rate. The threshold for "invitation" is much softer than direct Jesus-naming. Lived play that uses cost-vocabulary in any form will elicit cruciform register from responsive characters without needing explicit theological framing.

**What the data does NOT support:**

- That the formula vs prose decomposition is settled. Inconclusive at N=3.
- That the invitation gradient generalizes uniformly across all characters. Steven's low-responsiveness is a real outlier; not all non-pastoral characters respond to invitation equally.
- That production toggle should be made permanent. The data supports doing so on every measured dimension, but the user's lived play remains the test surface no rubric can fully proxy.

## Decision-shifting implications

1. **The formula-only configuration (`f5c537a`) can stay shipped.** Empirical case for it is now claim-tier confirmed across frame, drift, humor, theology, and multi-turn.
2. **The cross-bearing reauthor (`b9d6d18` + `6d2413a` + `20c690a`) is doing real work** on responsive characters at responsive invitation strengths. Specifically: it lifts soft-invited replies above where they were prior, on characters whose anchors carry the substrate. Steven would likely not have benefited; Aaron and Darren clearly do.
3. **The 0410 capacity correction is now claim-tier confirmed.** "Cold-probe Mode-C measures cold baseline, not capacity" should remain the methodological discipline. Cross-bearing in non-pastoral characters is context-elicited; the elicitation can be soft cost-vocabulary, not just explicit Jesus-naming.

## Confounds and caveats

- **N=2-3 per cell** at the cross-bearing gradient. Direction is solid; magnitudes (the 0.17 / 0.58 / 0.83 numbers for Aaron) could shift ±0.20 at characterized tier. The pattern (cold < soft < full-invited) is robust.
- **Single-character integrated session (Exp 10).** The 5/5 PASS is on Aaron specifically. A second character's integrated session (Pastor Rick or Darren) would tighten the claim. Out of scope at this budget.
- **Drift check used only Mode A grading on a small Aaron-only window.** Cross-character drift checks (especially on Steven, who showed atypical low-responsiveness) would surface character-specific drift if any exists.
- **The "invited" probe in Exp 1-2 named Jesus directly** (*"Tell me what Jesus means to you"*). The soft-invitation gradient (Exp 3) used cost-vocabulary without Jesus-naming. The two findings together establish the gradient; either alone would not.
