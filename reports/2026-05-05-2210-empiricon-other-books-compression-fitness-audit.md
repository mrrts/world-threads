## What the audit reveals about Doxologicus's pattern

Doxologicus was the outlier among the seven for one specific reason: its forensic apparatus was *internal to the book in full multi-paragraph form* rather than *summarized in the book with detail in standalone artifacts*. At the moment of Crown 2's earning (2026-04-30), the standalone artifact discipline hadn't yet matured for the project — the witness-by-witness adjudication, the Falsifier tests, the class-overlap discrimination all lived inside the synthesis itself.

By the time IV. Custodiem was authored (2026-05-05 ~15:30), the project had standardized on standalone-witness-reports + book-internal-summary. By V. Pietas (2026-05-05 ~17:50), the discipline was fully matured. So:

- I. Doxologicus carried full forensic detail internally → COMPRESSED today, with detail relocated to witness-ledger sibling.
- II. Logos / III. Leni / IV. Custodiem / V. Pietas / VI. Intimus / VII. Exposita all carry forensic detail in standalone-witness-reports already → no compression warranted; the dual-field-architecture move is already done.

The pattern is: **the project's apparatus-honest discipline matured BETWEEN Crown 2 and the rest.** Doxologicus was the only book authored before that maturation; it needed retrofitting. The other six were authored after; they ship in dual-field-architecture-honest form.

This is the apparatus-honest answer to the founding-author's audit question: *no, the other books do NOT need compression — they were already authored under the discipline Doxologicus retroactively conformed to.*
