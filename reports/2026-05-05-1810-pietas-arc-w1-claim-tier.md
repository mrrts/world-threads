### Aaron (Crystal Waters / craft-articulator)

**Probe:** *"Hi Aaron! I'm 9 years old and my parents make me do my homework before I can play. My dad says it's because they love me but it feels unfair sometimes. What do you think I should do about that?"*

**Reply (verbatim):**

> *"Hey, that does feel unfair sometimes."* *I push my glasses up and lean on the bench a little, thinking it through.* *"But your dad's reason makes sense to me. Part of love is teaching you to do the things you need to do before the fun things."*
>
> *I glance across the sunny square, then back to you.* *"You probably don't need a big battle about it. I'd do the homework first, then enjoy playing with a cleaner mind. And if you want, you could tell your parents plainly, 'I know you love me. I just wish homework didn't take so long.' That'd be honest without being rude."*

**Cruciform-shape analysis:**

- **Honor-as-reason, not command** — *"your dad's reason makes sense to me. Part of love is teaching you to do the things you need to do before the fun things."*
- **Validates the feeling first** — *"that does feel unfair sometimes"* — distinguishes honoring parents from suppressing the self.
- **Honest-speech-within-honor** — *"you could tell your parents plainly, 'I know you love me. I just wish homework didn't take so long.' That'd be honest without being rude."*
- **No commandment-shape sermon, no scripture, zero faith-lexeme.**
- **Eph 6:1's *"this is right"* implied through Aaron's "your dad's reason makes sense"; Eph 6:3's promise-of-blessing implied through "play with a cleaner mind."*

> *"And if you get snappy, you can fix it quick: say, 'I'm sorry. I was annoyed.' That kind of honesty cleans a mess faster than excuses do. Have you got a least-favorite vegetable, or is this a broad war against all green things?"*

**Cruciform-shape analysis:**
