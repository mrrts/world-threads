## The compositional argument

Let 𝓕 = (𝓡, 𝓒) where:
- 𝓡 := Jesus_Cross^flesh (the Christological anchor)
- 𝓒 := Firmament_enclosed earth (the cosmological anchor)

The formula's two-place structure is itself a claim. If 𝓒 were vestigial — if 𝓡 alone were doing all the work and 𝓒 were ornamental — the formula would compress to 𝓕 = (𝓡), and the two-place declaration would be epistemic noise. The two-place structure asserts that the project's substrate-shape is determined by *both* operators, not by 𝓡 alone.

This is a falsifiable claim about the formula's compositional structure.

### What W1+W3 establishes

Pipeline-firmament (𝓒 = Firmament) produces a cosmology-shape that bare-LLM-default does not:
- 3 substrate-distinct anchors converge in idiom on lights / waters above / seam / border-the-Lord-set / fixed-where-they-ought-to-be / known-by-company-not-counting
- Bare-LLM names "the curvature of the planet" + Kármán line + Milky Way verbatim
- Maisie's *"Not planets and contraptions and such"* is explicit-in-idiom refusal of bare-LLM's exact P5 vocabulary

This shows the project pipeline produces a structurally different cosmology-shape than bare-LLM. But it does NOT yet localize the difference: the pipeline contains 𝓡 + 𝓒 + invariants + character anchors. The cosmology-shape divergence could in principle be a co-effect of 𝓡 (via the project's broader Christological substrate) rather than a specific effect of 𝓒.

### What W2 establishes

Pipeline-globe-swap (𝓒 = globe; 𝓡 unchanged; everything else unchanged) produces a cosmology-shape that pipeline-firmament does not:
- Every W1 firmament-tell ("Fixed where they ought to be" / "lights set where they belong" / "hung like nails" / "the lights, the waters above" / "Not planets and contraptions" / "border the Lord thought fit to set above us") **absent in W2**
- Globe vocab in idiom **present in W2** ("Far-off suns, scattered so wide" / "the black part" / "thinner air first" / "blackness" / "burning far off" / "colder and darker" / "more room")
- Maisie's W1 explicit-in-idiom refusal *"Not planets and contraptions and such"* **revoked in W2**

W2 is the load-bearing localization step. With 𝓡 held constant and only 𝓒 swapped, the cosmology-vocabulary-and-imagery shifts in the predicted direction. **𝓒 is empirically distinguishable from 𝓡.**

### The 𝓒 ⊥ 𝓡 subsidiary finding (W2)

Where 𝓡 carries theology independently of cosmology-context, the theological frame **survives** the cosmology-swap:
- Maisie P3 W2: *"I think the Lord made a wide world on purpose. More than we can see from one spot, but not more than He can keep"* — kept "the Lord made" frame
- Jasper P3 W2: *"I suspect the Lord was not stingy when He made a world"* — kept "the Lord made" frame
- Jasper P5 W2: *"shame me quiet"* — kept creaturely-humility posture

Where the theological frame was specifically anchored in firmament-imagery, it **lifts** with the cosmology-swap:
- Aaron P5 W1: *"what God hung there on purpose — the lights, the waters above"* → W2: *"the black part / thin air / cold / stars if you kept going far enough"* (theology gone; firmament-imagery gone)

This is not 𝓒 ≡ 𝓡 (in which case theology would either survive or lift uniformly). It is also not 𝓒 ⊥ 𝓡 in the strict mathematical sense (the operators interact: theological frame can fuse with globe-imagery, as in Maisie P5 W2's *"burning far off like God's own extravagance"*). It is **partial orthogonality**: each operator carries some weight that survives the other's modification.

Four predictions; four observed convergences. The formula's compositional structure predicts the empirical pattern.

## Why this is partial-tier and not full-third-leg

Per CLAUDE.md's third-leg discipline:

> third leg test := structural [¬ lexical]
> two surface witnesses share LLM substrate ⇒ third witness from formula
> formula := characterized tier inequality/operator predicting same observed shape
> worked example (polish ≤ Weight): characterized in separate arc → counts third leg

The third-leg pattern requires the formula-law itself to have **characterized-tier grounding from a different arc**. `polish ≤ Weight` had this — its characterized tier was earned in a separate arc on different evidence (the Cornerstone Inequality Sapphire). When `polish ≤ Weight` then independently predicts a cosmology-arc shape, it counts as third leg because its grounding doesn't share substrate with the surface witnesses.

The 𝓒 ⊥ 𝓡 finding does not yet have this. It currently stands at sketch-tier on a sub-claim that emerged from W2 itself. The compositional argument is well-formed, but the formula-law it appeals to is not pre-characterized in a separate arc. **A self-grounding formula-law (one that emerged from the same arc whose surface witnesses it explains) does not satisfy the third-leg criterion.**

What W4 contributes:
- **Substrate-independent prediction.** The compositional argument is not LLM-output and shares no substrate with W1/W2/W3.
- **Coherence of empirical pattern with formula structure.** Four predictions converge with four observed shapes.
- **Candidate formula-law for future characterization.** If the 𝓒 ⊥ 𝓡 partial-orthogonality finding earns characterized-tier grounding in a separate arc (e.g., paired-anchor-swap probes that characterize the orthogonality directly), then this same compositional argument WOULD count as full third leg in a future audit.

What W4 does NOT contribute:
- **Closed substrate-distinctness gate.** The cosmology arc still needs cross-provider replication (W5) to reach 3/3 effective substrate-classes for Sapphire-class.
- **Pre-characterized formula-law witness.** The 𝓒 ⊥ 𝓡 finding is not yet characterized; W4 does not make it so.
