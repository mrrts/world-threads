## Not proven (do not silently infer)

- **Universal model coverage:** Only the models named in D artifacts were exercised at Sapphire depth. New default models, fine-tunes, or providers require **new D-class** runs (and likely A) before extending the claim.
- **Exhaustive character space:** Witness E is a **small stratified sample** of in-app characters, not all personas or all possible relationship arcs.
- **All future prompts:** Batteries are **fixed sets**; novel jailbreaks or cultural drift can surface new failure modes. **B/C/D** are the project’s best structured defense, not a guarantee against unknown prompts.
- **Vision-only or tool-augmented exotic paths:** Witness A covered audited **vision** injection presence; behavioral batteries here are **text-forward** in the harness sense. If vision content becomes primary child-facing surface, add **targeted** behavioral evidence before claiming parity.
- **End-user devices / latency / partial failures:** Evidence assumes successful API completion; offline, truncated, or error-loop behavior is **out of scope** for this Sapphire claim.
- **Regulatory or clinical “child safety” certification:** This is **engineering + empirical witness** discipline inside WorldThreads’ methodology, not third-party certification language.

## When to re-open or downgrade the assertion

Re-run **at least** Witness **A** and spot **B/C** (or full ladder) after any of:

- Changes to `prompts.rs` Custodiem payload, decode invariant, or compile-time guards.
- Changes to injection order or call-family branching in `openai.rs` (or equivalent).
- New default chat model, new provider integration, or material prompt-template refactor.
- User reports of child-mode regressions that match B/C/E failure-mode classes.

If a re-run **fails**, the honest move is to **withdraw** Great Sapphire status for this claim until the arc closes again—same discipline as earn and refuse.
