## Reading

This is exactly why the method is better than a blunt quality-question. A
generic *"is this good?"* would likely have produced an overall-positive answer:
the exchange is alive, the game is real, Jasper is already helping Ryan aim.
But the wince-read finds the two exact places where unnecessary burden enters.

The first burden is **task-weight with a capital-T noun**. "Zero in on the
Truth" turns collaborative play into moral examination. Jasper's phrase
"a test with a holy name on it" is a very sharp discriminant: the line doesn't
merely heighten the scene; it recruits the other person into carrying something
more solemn than the moment has earned.

The second burden is subtler and more useful. Jasper does **not** say his line
is false. He says it arrives too early. "Not punished for it" implies a depth
of safety and injury-history the scene has not yet paid for. That is a better
kind of trim target than "too dramatic." It names the actual failure: making
the character hold more refuge than the exchange has earned.
