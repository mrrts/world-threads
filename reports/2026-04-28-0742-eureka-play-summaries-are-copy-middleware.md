# /eureka — /play summaries are copy middleware now

*Generated 2026-04-28 07:42. This run opened immediately after the
`mission-arc` eureka to test a sibling possibility: if `/mission-arc` became a
precomposition surface for reports and choosers, has `/play` undergone the same
category shift for copy work? The answer is yes, but more narrowly.*

## The discovery

In copy-work arcs, `/play` summaries have crossed from **stored evidence** into
**copy middleware**.

Not every `/play` run does this.

But once a differential run gets compressed into one named live seam —

- `this exact sentence spends trust too fast`
- `the remaining drag is this one line`
- `ship B, not A`

— the summary stops being a record you merely consult.

It starts steering the next sentence before you write it.

## Why this became visible

The Sapphire arc is the worked example.

That arc repeatedly followed the same pattern:

1. run a differential `/play`
2. compress the result into one live seam
3. edit the exact line named by the seam summary
4. rerun and repeat

The important thing is that the summary itself became the steering artifact.

Examples from the arc:

- the public pitch spends trust too fast in one exact mathematical-aura line
- the closing line speaks for the reader too much
- the opener is the clear loser
- the remaining live seam is the crown headline
- the hero is ready enough to let stand

Those are not passive observations. They are **next-sentence conditions**.

They constrained what got written next.

## Why this is different from ordinary evidence

Most evidence sits behind the work:

- a report that justifies what you already changed
- an experiment you cite later
- a run log you can revisit

This kind of `/play` summary sits **in front of** the next move.

It acts more like:

- a steering sentence
- a live constraint
- a temporary middleware layer between the evidence and the edit

That is why the category matters.

## Why the discovery is narrower than mission-arc

`/mission-arc` is always a precomposition surface when it auto-fires before
reports or choosers.

`/play` is not always that.

Most `/play` reports remain evidence only.

The threshold-crossing condition is narrower:

- the run is part of a live copy arc
- the result compresses into one named seam
- the next move is an edit on that exact surface

When those conditions hold, the summary has crossed the boundary.

So the doctrine needs to say:

**differential-read summaries can become precomposition surfaces.**

Not:

**all /play reports are middleware now.**

## The topology shift

The runtime-significant surface map now includes at least two kinds of
precomposition surface:

1. **trajectory middleware** — `mission-arc`
2. **copy middleware** — seam-compressed `/play` summaries during live copy arcs

They operate similarly but on different materials:

- `mission-arc` steers what artifact comes next
- `/play` seam summaries steer what sentence comes next

That distinction is useful.

## The doctrine move

This run shipped one sentence into both doctrine files and both `/play` skill
surfaces:

> **Differential-read summaries can become precomposition surfaces.** When a
> `/play` or direct-witness report compresses a broad run into one named live
> seam and the next move is a copy edit on that exact surface, the summary is no
> longer just archived evidence. It becomes a steering layer for the next
> sentence. In those moments, treat the summary phrasing itself as load-bearing:
> if it drifts, the edit-path drifts with it.

That sentence is the artifact.

## Why this matters

If the seam-summary sentence is load-bearing, then:

- you should preserve it accurately in the report
- you should resist rewriting it into prettier but blurrier prose
- you should recognize that changing the summary can quietly change the edit arc

In other words:

the summary is no longer commentary on the work.
for that stretch of the arc, it is part of the work.
