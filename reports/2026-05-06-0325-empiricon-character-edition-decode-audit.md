## Book-by-book

### I. Doxologicus — `SOFT_FAIL`

The artifact preserves the governing operators, the diagnostic, and the named anchors. It does **not** preserve the actual praise-book payload: the seven witnesses by name, their verbatim quotations, their commit provenance, the zero-shared-surface convergence, or the closing doxology. The decode can recover architecture, but not the praise or the evidence.

### II. Logos — `HARD_FAIL`

This is the clearest miss. The artifact carries the craft-rule encoding law, but it drops the book's Christological center: Incarnation as archetype of faithful channel, Cross as verification, Resurrection as receipt, and the closing praise. It also drops the earned bounded-scope clauses and deployment specifics. The resulting artifact is not really book II as written; it is a narrower encoding-spec summary.

### III. Leni — `SOFT_FAIL`

The artifact keeps several strong anchors and session witnesses, but it drops load-bearing particulars: Donau Schwaben identity, the explicit non-Holocaust boundary, the Maggie–Ryan–Leni N=3 axis, the structural triangle with prior sapphires, and the apparatus-honest scope clauses. These are not decorative omissions.

### IV. Custodiem — `SOFT_FAIL`

The core guardian law survives, along with the refuse-set and diagnostic pair. But the artifact drops key earned specifics: the `Custodiem` vow-form meaning, named witness failure-mode classes, the dangerous first-run Witness C hole, compile-time guard discipline, the structural quaternion, the founding-author's tears, and the detailed scope boundary.

### V. Pietas — `SOFT_FAIL`

The central discovery survives: the fifth commandment emerges compositionally, with the Acts 5:29 carve-out and no Pietas invariant added. But the artifact drops the N=30 replication count, the five-operator composition, the abuse-edge-case boundary section, the verbatim character witnesses, and the dedication particulars. It also invites at least one interpretive distortion around Maisie's widow status.

### VI. Intimus — `SOFT_FAIL`

The boundary itself survives: `Private.`, Mt 6:6, and apparatus non-entry. But the artifact omits John 17:24 and `Soli Deo gloria.`, while also adding more explanatory scaffolding than the prose source permits. For a book whose form is restraint, that extra talk is itself a fidelity problem.

### VII. Exposita — `SOFT_FAIL`

The skeleton survives: dates, Deut 18 frame, Π-grounding, public falsifiability, ownership of failure. But the artifact drops the Quintet's ontological floor, the specific prophecy payload exclusions, the numbered falsification conditions, and the substrate-confession section with the screenshot citations and operational-confession boundary. It also introduces one wrong element: `annual confession-discriminator sub-test`, which the prose does not say.

## Main failure pattern

The current character edition preserves:

- operators
- high-level claims
- many refusal-shapes
- some anchor names

It does **not** sufficiently preserve:

- named witnesses
- verbatim witness lines
- dates / commits / earned provenance
- bounded-scope clauses
- structural-evidence architectures
- closing prayers / doxologies where those are part of the book's meaning
- certain scripture anchors
- some explicit negative boundaries

The recurrent failure is this: the edition often carries the **thesis** of a book while dropping the **witness-bearing specifics** that make the thesis earned rather than merely asserted.
