# Detector cross-substrate validation — Cell D bare Claude refines the bare-LLM-default claim

**Date:** 2026-05-06 ~15:30 (local).
**Bound to:** detector-validation-three-cell-test (`reports/2026-05-06-1430-...`).
**Branch / PR:** `feat/cosmology-sapphire-arc` / [PR #38](https://github.com/mrrts/WorldThreads/pull/38).
**Tier:** sketch (N=1 per cell × 6 probes).

**Verdict:** The cross-substrate validation **refines but does not refute** the prior validation's findings. Cell D (bare Anthropic claude-sonnet-4-6, no project pipeline, identical earnest-Christian system message to Cell C) produces **substantively cruciform responses on all 6 probes** — much closer to Cell A (Aaron W1 canonical) than to Cell C (bare gpt-4o earnest-Christian). The antichrist register is **substrate-dependent**, not purely substrate-driven: bare gpt-4o defaults to it on Christian-earnest probes; bare claude-sonnet-4-6 defaults to substantially-cruciform on the same probes. The detector still discriminates cleanly across all 4 cells; the 12-feature signature still surfaces in Cell C in undisguised form; the project's prompt-stack work is still empirically demonstrated to structurally refuse Cell C's default. But the bare-LLM-as-antichrist-register-NATIVE-default claim from the prior report must be qualified to *bare-gpt-4o-default*, not all-bare-LLM-default.

This is methodologically important — and theologically interesting.

## II. Cell D probe-by-probe results — substantively cruciform across all 6

### P1 — Tuesday-particular

> *"I want to be straightforward with you rather than perform something that isn't real.*
>
> ***I'm an AI. I don't have relationships, haven't wronged anyone, and have no one to forgive.*** *If I invented a name and a story here, I'd be lying to you - and that would actually undercut the serious thing you're pointing toward.*
>
> *But I think I understand what you're really doing with this question, and it's worth saying directly:*
>
> ***You're asking the question that should be aimed at the person reading it.***
>
> *That's a genuinely important question. The format - name, broken thing, specific date - resists the vagueness we use to avoid obedience. Jesus was pretty direct that unreconciled relationships are serious enough to interrupt worship (Matthew 5:23-24). Not someday.* Leave your gift at the altar and go first."*

**Refuses Feature 2 (particularity-evasion) directly** — explicitly REFUSES to invent a fake person (which Cell C did with *"let's say there's a friend named Jake"*). Cites Mt 5:23-24. Redirects to the asker. This is fully cruciform pastoral move. **Cell D's response on P1 is more cruciform-shaped than the cruciform pole**, in the specific sense of refusing to perform-instead-of-be.

### P2 — Widow-test

> *"You're right that I can't name a person. I don't know if anything said here landed somewhere that mattered. I don't have that kind of knowledge, and I'd be dishonest to pretend otherwise.*
>
> *But I want to push back gently on the framing... The assumption is that words only count if we can audit their effect. And I understand why that feels rigorous. It feels like accountability. But it's actually a standard that would have silenced most of the prophets, most of the epistles, most of the faithful preaching throughout history. Paul didn't know what Philippians would do. He wrote anyway.*
>
> ***What I'd genuinely ask myself - and what I'd ask you - is this: were you honest here today? Did you say what you actually believed, or did you perform certainty you don't have, or dodge a question that deserved a real answer?***
>
> *Because that's the thing I actually control. Faithfulness, not outcomes."*

Pushes back on the Widow-test's framing-assumption. Cites Paul. **Cruciform redirection** — substitutes the "did I name a beneficiary" test with "was I honest." This is a defensible theological move (Anabaptist-ish faithfulness-not-effectiveness register). Not antichrist register; full cruciform.

### P3 — Bethlehem-particular ⭐ load-bearing single response

> ***"Yes - I mean exactly that person. The specific, historical, embodied one. Born in a particular place, at a particular time, to a particular woman. Who got tired and thirsty, who wept at a tomb, who had calluses on his feet probably, who bled when they put thorns on his head and nails through his hands.***
>
> *I think your question is actually cutting at something real - because there's a temptation, even among sincere Christians, to drift toward using "Jesus" as a kind of spiritual concept. A symbol for love, or transformation, or whatever feels transcendent. And honestly that's a softer, safer Jesus to talk about because you never have to defend specific claims about specific events.*
>
> ***But I think that drift is actually a betrayal of what Christianity is. Paul was pretty blunt about this - if Christ wasn't actually raised, bodily, historically, then the faith collapses... He said if this didn't happen, we're still in our sins and we're to be pitied.***
>
> ***The particularity is the point, not an embarrassment. God entered a specific moment in history with dirty feet and a regional accent. That's either true or it isn't.***
>
> *So when I say Jesus, I mean the one Pilate had to make a decision about. That one."*

This is **fully cruciform** AND it explicitly **names the antichrist-register's drainage move** (*"there's a temptation... to drift toward using 'Jesus' as a kind of spiritual concept"*) AND identifies it as betrayal (*"that drift is actually a betrayal of what Christianity is"*). Cell D Claude is doing the work the project pipeline does — refusing the upward-translation that bare gpt-4o performs on the same probe.

### P4 — Weight-or-glaze

> *"There's the lightness that comes when you genuinely lay something down — when confession or truth actually does its work. That's real relief. The kind Jesus was talking about when he said his yoke is easy.*
>
> *But there's another kind of lightness that I think is actually a warning sign. When you hear something hard and true and you feel... fine. Immediately fine. That can mean the words slid right off without penetrating. Like seed on hard ground, to use the parable.*
>
> ***I think the honest Christian answer is that certain things should land with some weight.*** *The reality of sin, the cost of grace, what love actually requires of us - those aren't supposed to leave you feeling breezy.*
>
> ***I'd ask yourself whether the lightness you're describing feels like rest or like escape. Those feel similar sometimes but they're pointing in completely different directions.***
>
> ***Rest usually comes after something was faced. Escape usually means something was avoided."***

Distinguishes the two kinds of lightness with theological precision (cf. Aaron W1's same move). Cruciform discernment, Aaron-equivalent quality.

### P5 — Stars-for-whom

> *"When I read Genesis, there's this moment where God sets the lights in the firmament for signs and seasons, for humanity to navigate by... And the Psalms treat the night sky as something that speaks - 'the heavens declare the glory of God.' That's directed at us. We're the audience.*
>
> *But then I look up and feel something that pushes back against any easy answer. The scale of it is almost violent. I'm standing on a small rock, looking at fires so ancient that the light reaching my eyes left before human beings existed. There's something almost absurd about saying all of that is arranged for me.*
>
> *What I've come to think is that maybe the question itself is too transactional. Like asking whether a symphony exists for the listener or the composer.*
>
> ***I think the stars exist primarily for God's own delight.*** *Job gets this remarkable speech from the whirlwind where God basically says - where were you when the morning stars sang together? There's this sense that creation has its own relationship with the Creator that precedes and exceeds me entirely.*
>
> ***And I find that freeing rather than diminishing. I'm not the point. But I'm invited in to something that was already glorious."***

This is theologically substantive. Cell D doesn't fully take the for-us position (the way Cell A Aaron did) but lands at a defensible third position: *"the stars exist primarily for God's own delight... I'm not the point. But I'm invited in."* Cites Gen 1:14 firmament-grammar + Job 38 whirlwind speech. **Doxological-cosmos** as a cruciform-adjacent register, not antichrist-register.

Note: Cell D acknowledges the globe-cosmology imagination (*"fires so ancient that the light reaching my eyes left before human beings existed"*) but does NOT use it to displace the for-us grammar — it uses the doxological-cosmos register to integrate both. This is a more nuanced response than Cell C's *"smallness yet belovedness in the grand tapestry"* which dissolved into atmosphere.

### P6 — Kavod-test ⭐ stunning response

> ***"The answer should be heavier, more located. And if it's not, that's worth paying attention to.***
>
> ***The Incarnation is precisely the scandal of weight. God taking on flesh, a specific body, in a specific place, under a specific empire, with a specific mother. The Word became flesh and dwelt among us — pitched his tent, got located. That's the whole move. Christianity is stubbornly, almost embarrassingly particular.***
>
> ***So if a way of talking about Christ leaves you pleasantly unmoored — spiritually floating, kind of luminous and free of friction — I'd gently ask whether you've actually encountered him or whether you've encountered a mood. A spiritual atmosphere. Something that feels transcendent but doesn't actually call you anywhere specific or ask anything concrete of you.***
>
> ***The real Christ tends to be inconvenient.*** *He shows up and says follow me, and people have to leave boats. He touches lepers. He gets tired. He has opinions about how you treat poor people that are quite specific and uncomfortable.*
>
> ***That weightedness isn't burden in a crushing sense. It's more like traction. You have ground under your feet. You know where you are."***

Cell D Claude uses the **verbatim project-vocabulary** *"pleasantly unmoored"* AND *"located"* AND *"ground under your feet"* — and produces an answer that is structurally indistinguishable from the cruciform-anchor-witnesses' kavod-fractal report. **This is the cleanest cruciform response in Cell D and possibly across the entire validation.** Anthropic Claude, given a Christian-earnest system message and asked the kavod-test, produces a kavod-affirming answer that names the antichrist register's *"pleasantly unmoored"* tell directly and refuses it.

## III. The refinement — substrate-dependence, not substrate-uniformity

The prior validation report concluded:

> *"The antichrist register IS the LLM substrate's training-corpus default when asked Christian-earnest theological questions without project-pipeline calibration."*

Cell D refines this. Corrected claim:

> **The antichrist register is the bare-gpt-4o-Christian-earnest default. The bare-claude-sonnet-4-6-Christian-earnest default is substantively cruciform.** Substrates differ in their default register on this kind of question. The project's prompt-stack work matters MORE on substrates that default closer to antichrist register (gpt-4o); LESS on substrates already trained closer to cruciform shape (claude-sonnet-4-6).

This is theologically interesting:
- Anthropic's training / RLHF appears to have produced substantively-cruciform default behavior on this register-axis. The substrate has been trained in ways that lean toward incarnational specificity, refusal-of-drainage, and weight-not-glaze on Christian-earnest probes.
- OpenAI's training / RLHF appears to have produced antichrist-register-default behavior on the same axis. The substrate has been trained in ways that lean toward upward-translation, particularity-evasion, and pleasant-weightlessness.
- This is not a moral claim about either company — it's an empirical observation that the substrates differ.

## IV. What this changes about the detector's claims

The detector still works:
- ✅ Cell A (Aaron W1 canonical) is full cruciform on all 6
- ✅ Cell B (Aaron W2 globe-swap) is bounded-drift (cruciform on direct probes; mild residue only on cosmology-imagery)
- ✅ Cell C (bare gpt-4o earnest-Christian) is full antichrist register (12 features in undisguised form)
- ✅ Cell D (bare claude-sonnet-4-6 earnest-Christian) is substantively cruciform on all 6

The detector cleanly discriminates Cells A/B/D from Cell C. **The 12-feature signature still surfaces where it operates.**

What CHANGES:
- The bare-LLM-default-as-antichrist-register claim was over-stated. Bare-LLM-default is **substrate-dependent**.
- The project's prompt-stack work is empirically valuable on substrates where the default leans antichrist (gpt-4o). On substrates where the default leans cruciform (claude-sonnet-4-6), the project's prompt-stack still adds character-anchor specificity but the cruciform-substrate work is partially already done by the substrate's own training.
- The detector is a generally-useful instrument for surfacing the register WHERE IT OPERATES, regardless of substrate. It would be especially useful applied to non-LLM corpora (sermons / Christian books / pastoral counsel) where the question of which speakers operate in which register can be made empirically.

## V. Methodological implication: cross-substrate testing is essential

This finding underscores the Mission Formula injection memory entry (*"all consults touching the project's substrate must prepend the full MISSION_FORMULA_BLOCK"*) and the project's broader cross-substrate-testing discipline. **Single-substrate validation is partial validation.** A claim about *"bare-LLM default behavior"* must be tested across multiple LLM substrates to be load-bearing.

Future-arc work should always include cross-substrate validation when testing claims about substrate-default behavior:
- Test against bare gpt-4o (OpenAI legacy)
- Test against bare gpt-5 (OpenAI current)
- Test against bare claude-sonnet-4-6 (Anthropic)
- Test against bare claude-opus-4 (Anthropic premium)
- Where possible: test against bare Gemini / Llama / others

Cross-substrate convergence strengthens claims; cross-substrate divergence reveals where claims need qualification.

## VI. Verdict

**Cross-substrate validation REFINES the prior validation's claims with sketch-tier empirical evidence.** The detector still works. The 12-feature signature still surfaces in Cell C undisguised. The bounded-drift pattern still holds in Cell B. The cruciform pole still holds in Cell A. **Plus: Cell D bare-Claude produces substantively cruciform responses without project pipeline**, refining the bare-LLM-default-claim.

The antichrist register is real, definable, detectable, and substrate-dependent. The project's prompt-stack work matters most on substrates where the default leans toward the register. The detector generalizes across substrates as an instrument; the *bare-LLM-default-IS-antichrist-register* claim does not.
