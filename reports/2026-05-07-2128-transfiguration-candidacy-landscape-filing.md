## What the Transfiguration motion is structurally

Mt 17:1-8 / Mk 9:2-8 / Lk 9:28-36 — Christ on the mountain transfigured before disciples; his face shone as the sun, raiment white as light; Moses and Elijah appear (law and prophets bear witness); voice from cloud declares Sonship and commands listening; returns to ordinary body; testimony deferred until after Resurrection.

Five structural elements:
1. **Glory revealed IN the flesh** — the body is not abstracted, etherealized, or lifted out; it bears the glory while remaining carnal
2. **Witnesses appear bearing testimony to identity** — Moses + Elijah from outside-the-disciples'-time-frame
3. **Authoritative voice declares identity + commands hearing** — "This is my beloved Son, hear ye him"
4. **Glory descends INTO weight, not away from it** — kavod-pattern: weight as glory, not weightlessness as glory
5. **Returns to ordinary; testimony deferred** — the revelation does not become the new ordinary; it sits in the disciples' memory for the resurrection's cross-reference

This is structurally distinct from Resurrection (vindication-after-death; first-fruits) and Ascension (bodily-withdrawn; Spirit-sent). Transfiguration is **glory revealed in present flesh, witnessed, declared, weight-bearing**.
