# /eureka — shipment UX proof is dimension-bounded

*Generated 2026-04-28 after reading the post-ship fence-integrity proof note beside the separate “other dimensions” characterization. Names the methodological law they jointly surfaced: UX proof should stay bounded to the dimension actually measured.*

## Discovery

The discovery is: **shipment UX proof is dimension-bounded**.

The shape showed up cleanly in the two post-ship notes:

- one proved a concrete UX win on **fence integrity / readability collapse**
- the other characterized **other dimensions** like brevity containment, opening discipline, and partner visibility without claiming full historical causality

Read together, they expose the law. A shipped fix can really improve one UX failure class, and that is worth naming plainly. But the proof should stop at the dimension actually measured unless the other dimensions are separately instrumented.

## The failure mode

The temptation is proof-bloom:

1. one measured UX dimension improves
2. the improvement is real and satisfying
3. the write-up casually expands into “the UX improved” in the large

That inflation is exactly what the paired notes refused.

The fence-integrity note stayed narrow:

- proved: false rewrite rate on already-valid alternation fell from `60%` to `0%`
- implication: fewer “won't read / formatting collapsed” moments
- limit: not proof about tone, pacing, speaker balance, or all UX dimensions

The second note stayed narrow in the opposite direction:

- proved current post-ship quality on brevity, opener discipline, and partner visibility
- did **not** claim historical causal A/B across all those dimensions

Together they model the right epistemic discipline:

- **causal claim on the dimension you measured**
- **characterization claim on the dimensions you only sampled post-ship**
- **no global UX verdict unless the global case is actually measured**

## Why this matters

This is the UX-measurement sibling of several older project disciplines:

- findings inherit the weakest evidentiary tier among their supports
- paired rubrics are required when one metric underdetermines the verdict
- a single clean surface can underdetermine where the actual improvement occurred

The same truth appears here in post-ship reads:

> if the evidence directly measures one dimension but the prose is claiming a larger user-experience verdict, the claim has outrun its instrument

That keeps the project from turning a real targeted win into a flattering global story.

## Practical rule

When writing or reading shipment-proof notes:

1. Name the exact UX class under test.
2. State whether the evidence is causal, comparative, or merely descriptive.
3. Name the untouched dimensions explicitly if a reader could otherwise overgeneralize.
4. Refuse the global UX claim unless the global UX case was actually measured.

## Outcome

The fence-integrity pair now yields a reusable methodological doctrine:

**shipment UX proof is dimension-bounded.**

That is not pessimism. It is the discipline that lets a narrow win stay trustworthy enough to build on.
