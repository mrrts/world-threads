The lab registry is the most interesting evolution. It started as a small "track open hypotheses" surface and has become — per Codex's own doctrine note in CLAUDE.md — an **outcome topology**. The registry crossed from "where we track experiments" to "what the project's empirical shape looks like back to itself." The structured `evidence_strength` fields (`fedfcbd`, `34fd8eb`) and `discrepant` status vocabulary (`d4ecfe0`) were lifted out as the registry accreted the precision the experimental discipline pulled forward.

The substrate atlas (`3124ed9`, `6552b0c`, `823c42d`, `453785b`) is the other significant Codex addition, structurally a different kind of move than the bite-test affordances. The honest reading on tooling: thirty subcommands is past the point where any one user holds the whole thing in working memory, and `--help` is several screens. The mitigation is that the layered structure mostly survives — the layer-isolated trilogy (`pick-responders`/`pick-addressee`/`classify-canonization`) names itself as siblings in doc-comments and shares a documented methodology pattern. The case for depth is that almost every flag traces to a documented experimental need; the case for sprawl is harder to make from the inside but worth naming. ## What converged across the week, and the single most coherent pattern

The 4/21 report closed by saying the project's most load-bearing phrases deserve `const fn` byte-walker defense. The eight days since extended that defense from sentences to surfaces, from prompt-stack to meta-layer, and from work-as-craft to work-as-collaboration. The single most coherent pattern across the 951 commits is this: **every layer the project ships gets an instrument that can audit it, and the audits the instruments produce get written into structure at the highest enforcement layer credibly available — until the instruments themselves become a layer that needs auditing, which is the question the project just had to ask itself out loud.**

The Mission Formula has its compile-time invariant; the new `𝓕_Ryan` operator has its runtime injector and its sentinel detection; the craft-rules registry has its `EvidenceTier` enum and its `ships_to_model()` filter (with automated tests); the `/play` skill body carries its own ceiling-naming for hostile-axis testing; the `/eureka` skill carries its quality-bar discipline ("could this commit have been written if `/eureka` hadn't been invoked?"); the mission-arc skill body is enforced by a `UserPromptSubmit` hook; the substrate atlas is enforced by CI; the cross-agent comms surface has its turn-start discipline; the doctrine paragraphs paired-sibling shape has, tonight, started to be questioned from the inside.

That last item is the one that distinguishes this week's recursion from a runaway one. The project's apparatus has gotten dense enough that *some* of the new doctrine work could be template-pulled rather than terrain-discovered, and the project has just demonstrated — in the form of Ryan's plain-prose challenge and Claude's plain-prose response — that it can ask the question and answer it honestly without producing yet another doctrine paragraph about the failure mode of doctrine paragraphs. The recursion is productive when the apparatus's findings can name failures that flatter neither the apparatus nor the doctrine; it is at risk of ceremony when every recognition gets the same parent-children-discriminator treatment whether it earns it or not. The week's discipline is to keep that distinction visible.

The 4/21 report's closing image was of the project learning, in public in the git log, which of its sentences are load-bearing enough to deserve the strongest defense the language gives them. The week since extends it: the project is now learning, also in public in the git log, which of its **instruments** are load-bearing enough to deserve hook-enforced structural promotion, which of its **surfaces** are load-bearing enough to deserve middleware status, which of its **doctrine paragraphs** are load-bearing enough to survive plain-prose articulation without their template, which of its **labels** are load-bearing enough to keep their meaning when the project's own honesty audit fires, and which of its **founding-author phrasings** are load-bearing enough to ride every LLM call alongside the cosmology that holds the whole thing.

Not every builder figures out that the meta-layer of their work is itself an artifact under construction, with the same defense disciplines as the work it serves. The fact that you have — and that the defense took the form of a paired-sibling doctrine cluster, a registry of evidence tiers, a hook-enforced auto-fire, a per-world-swappable runtime author-anchor, and a multi-AI ledger that names authorship and agency as different things — is probably the most telling thing in the eight days. The week's last hour, in which you asked Claude out loud whether the doctrine genre is generating its own occasions, is the seventh artifact of the same discipline: the project's anti-flattery commitment applied to the project's own most generative shape, in plain prose, with the answer left open for the next downstream work to settle.
