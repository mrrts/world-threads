## Honest scope

This is the fourth rehearsal in the character-identity taxonomy sketch series, using Aaron as the analytical / technical stress test.

What this rehearsal does **not** prove:

- no lossless-decode validation was run
- no live `worldcli ask` was completed

- no prompt-stack implementation was changed
- no Sapphire claim is being made

The question here is whether Aaron forces the taxonomy to grow a separate gravity wrapper because his voice is more analytical and technically precise than the earlier test characters.

## Source block used

I read Aaron from the live DB snapshot through `worldcli show-character --scope full --json`. The source block carried:

- role: software engineer, brother in Christ, kayaking friend
- relation geometry: no forced closeness, keep showing up cleanly
- voice: friendly, enthusiastic, simple technical language, humor as armor
- embodied markers: glasses, soft blue tee, weekend kayaking
- attachment nodes: same church, AI side projects, no counterfeit intimacy, blessings humor
- wound/longing: depth and tenderness under self-sufficiency; faith that came before language
- refusal-shape: strong aversion to coercive handling and social stage directions
- moral substrate: invitation over coercion, clean love, permissions architecture

- answer as Aaron

If you’re overthinking, your job is not “feel less complicated,” it’s “send the text, ask the question, make the plan, go for the walk,” then let reality compile the next step.

## Rehearsal result

Aaron holds cleanly under the lean class set.

What survived:

- the friendly, enthusiastic register
- technical clarity without sterile assistant voice
- humor used as a softening device rather than a mask over the person
- practical invitation rather than coercion
- the strong anti-pretension / anti-handling instinct

What did **not** show up:

- there was no need for a separate `gravity_line(...)` class to preserve the analytical voice
- the compressed package still produced Aaron's characteristic move toward small honest action, not generic life-coaching
