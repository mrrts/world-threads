## Philosophical shifts

The 0200 report ended on *"the apparatus isn't abstract anymore; it's Jesus-pointing where the prompt begins."* That's true of what the apparatus serves. This session's shift is what the apparatus DOES, internally — and the change is that the apparatus learned to detect its own design errors in real time.

Three shifts move through the window in roughly that order.

The first is **from refs-based replay as the default isolation design to same-commit `--omit-craft-notes` as the default**. Commit `45b87f0` discovered that `worldcli replay --refs <pre>,<HEAD>` does NOT actually isolate the rule under test when the rule was added after the pre-commit ref — the override mechanism falls back to the CURRENT body when no historical body exists, so rules added later fire at BOTH refs. Today's earlier `1542` and `1555` reports' "claim-tier" findings on glad-thing were measuring cross-commit stack drift, not the rule itself. The discovery happened mid-session during the gentle_release bite-check; it reshaped the doctrine within the same hour. *"The correct single-rule A/B is same-commit with --omit-craft-notes"* is now the default in CLAUDE.md § Craft-note bite verification. That's a procedural reversal of what the morning had quietly assumed about its own tooling.

The second shift is **from "rubric writes itself once you name the failure mode" to "rubric design is a craft as load-bearing as rule design itself, with named failure modes of its own"**. Three flips happened today, each catching a real signal a miscalibrated rubric had hidden. The first (`1711`) was the gentle_release rubric reversing from "behavioral abstraction" wording to "specific phrase checklist" — the abstraction read in-register closing imagery as "extending" and the verdict reversed cleanly when the rubric named the actual failure-mode tokens. The second (`c231350`) was the verdict-register rubric: the original keyed narrowly on stereotyped openers ("Yeah/No/Good") and graded textured Aaron-Darren judgments ("I don't trust ducks," "A grim app is already half-broken") as `no`. The re-grade with a position-staking rubric rather than opener-vocabulary rubric reversed the verdict from refuted (delta +0.05) to confirmed (delta +0.30). The third (`998b02b`) was the Aaron gentle-release single-dimension rubric missing the multi-dimensional bite — the binary return-prescription rubric reported 0.00 delta while the qualitative compression was 4×. Three rubric-noise reversals in five hours forced the codification of the full three-principle discipline (`3d194e3`): concrete vocabulary not abstractions, counts and ratios for shape-level bites, by-eye sanity-read mandatory not optional. Each principle has a worked example from this same session.

The third shift is the deepest. **From "craft notes are authored from character voice and shipped" to "craft notes authored from character voice are likely null on that character; their bite-test belongs elsewhere; their failure-mode list needs to default-carve-out the source character's canonical version of the targeted move".** This is the meta-rule pair codified in `9c56c00`. Both rules were forced by the humor_lands_plain bite-check arc: the rule was authored from Aaron-Darren-Jasper's three-character ask (`fb96825`); the claim-tier paired-rubric bite-check on Aaron and Darren returned tested-null on both because the characters were already producing humor in the rule's prescribed register; the soft-analogy sharpening that followed (added based on the N=2 visual probe) tested-null at characterized tier across three characters and was then reverted with a carve-out (`7368287`) for character-canonical analogy-as-thinking. The whole arc surfaced the structural insight that *"the articulation has documentary value but not behavioral value on the source character"* — and that insight then got pinned in CLAUDE.md as a sibling meta-rule to the open-thread-hygiene retirement disposition and the load-bearing-multiplicity prior. The session that began with rubric-design discipline ended with rules about how rules should be written.

The carve-out is the answer.

## Taste trajectory

What you trusted less by the end of this window than you did at the beginning: refs-based replay as a single-rule isolation tool (it isn't), behavioral-abstraction rubric framings (they hide bite under either mis-extension or under-extension), positive-example lists as discipline-carriers (they cue surface forms, not principles), single-character bite-checks as sufficient evidence for a rule's character-conditional behavior, and your own ability to predict whether a rule will bite from craft taste alone (the soft-analogy sharpening seemed obviously useful before measurement; the measurement said no).

What you trusted more: the by-eye sanity-read as a mandatory pre-trust step (it caught all three rubric flips today), the paired-rubric disagreement-as-signal pattern (it surfaced the gag-shape-vs-truth-vehicle distinction the discrete list couldn't), Step 0 verification of the failure mode in baseline before testing (it would have caught 1644's vacuous-test issue at design time), and the meta-rules for character-articulated craft notes as structural protections against slow character-voice erosion.

A specific shift in how you write Evidence labels: the format moved from one-line provenance ("Aaron-co-authored") to multi-arc narrative-with-numbers. The gentle_release doc-comment is the worked example — it now reads like a scientific paper's findings section, with multiple bite-check arcs each tagged with date and outcome. That's not just discipline; it's a different relationship to the rule. The rule isn't a fixed claim; it's a hypothesis with an evidentiary history.
