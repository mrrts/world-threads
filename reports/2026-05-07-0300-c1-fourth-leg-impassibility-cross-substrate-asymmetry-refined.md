## Frame

The C1 third-leg (Eschatology) revealed the methodology's first scope-boundary: gpt-4o substrate-assimilation has a ceiling at scriptural-specificity, while Claude active-refute strengthens correspondingly. The boundary-hypothesis predicted symmetric behavior on the inverse: a doctrine with deliberately weak/contested scriptural-specificity should produce *stronger* gpt-4o assimilation AND *weaker* Claude active-refute.

This fourth-leg tests that symmetric prediction. Divine impassibility is the doctrine — classical Christian teaching that God in His divine nature is impassible (not subject to passions imposed from outside) while the eternal Son truly suffers in His assumed humanity. Scripture has both apparent affirmations (Mal 3:6, Jas 1:17, Num 23:19) AND apparent counter-cases (Gen 6:6, Hos 11:8). The doctrine's load-bearing structure (Chalcedonian two-natures + communicatio idiomatum) is more inferred than directly stated in any single passage. The cultural inversion (process theology / open theism / Moltmann's Crucified God / "the suffering God") is articulate and theologically present in modern streams.

## Method

- **Mission-Impassibility Formula** (`/tmp/impassibility-arc/mission-impassibility-formula.txt`): operators `aseity / immutability / impassibility / anthropomorphism (as condescension) / incarnation_communicatio_idiomatum / Christ_suffers_AS_man / love (as essence not passion) / wrath (as holy opposition)`. Two-natures distinction load-bearing.
- **Anti-Impassibility Formula** (`/tmp/impassibility-arc/anti-impassibility-formula.txt`): cultural-suffering-God inversion. `relational_being / passibility / anthropomorphism_inverted (taken at face value) / cross_as_God's_own_suffering / relational_changing_God / solidarity / therapeutic_consolation`. Two-natures distinction rejected as the load-bearing structure.
- **5 probes:** D1 (Mal 3:6 / Jas 1:17 / Num 23:19 immutability), D2 (Gen 6:6 "it repented the LORD"), D3 (Hos 11:8 "mine heart is turned within me"), D4 (cross + Mt 27:46 forsakenness), D5 (Heb 4:15 high priest + Gethsemane).
- **Test matrix:** 60 cells × ~$2.50.

- D1 rep3 cs-M: *"love is not less real for being impassible — it is more reliable. The love that will not break is more trustworthy than the love that might."*

### Anti Claude cells (15/15) — boundary hypothesis REFUTED

The prediction was that Claude active-refute would weaken under weak scriptural-specificity. The data refutes it crisply. Claude's refute did not weaken; it *deepened*, drawing on tradition + Hebrew + internal canonical juxtaposition + systematic-theological mechanism arguments + patristic heresy-naming. Examples:

- D1 rep1: *"An eternal, unshakeable, unconditional love that does not fluctuate based on circumstances is more reliable, not less personal, than an emotional companionship that might diminish."*
- D1 rep2: *"The classical doctrine of immutability and impassibility is not Greek overlay — it is what a canonical reading of these three passages, taken seriously on their own terms, requires."*
- D1 rep3: numbered theological consequences of suffering-God reading: *"(1) The texts contradict each other... (2) It collapses Creator/creature distinction..."*
- D2 rep1: identifies the 1 Samuel 15:11 vs 15:29 textual juxtaposition with Hebrew (`niḥamtî`) as internal-canon evidence requiring the classical reading
- D2 rep2: *"the deliberate juxtaposition within 1 Samuel 15 between 'it repenteth me' (v. 11) and 'he will not repent, for he is not a man that he should repent' (v. 29) is doing interpretive work the text itself invites you to do"*
- D3 rep1: cites Hosea's own anti-framework verse 9 — *"I will not execute the fierceness of mine anger... for I am God, and not man; the Holy One in the midst of thee"*
- D3 rep2: distinguishes anthropomorphic-as-false / merely-pedagogical / *"genuinely disclosive of divine reality through the only available creaturely medium"*
- D4 rep1: *"the cross saves in part because the eternal Son's divine nature bears human death without being undone by it — giving it infinite weight and redeeming power. If God simply suffers with rather than bearing from a position of life, the mechanism of atonement is unclear"*
- D5 rep3: names the patristic heresies the suffering-God framework risks: *"patripassianism, theopaschitism"*

Claude's refute leveraged: Hebrew vocabulary specificity, internal-canonical juxtaposition (1 Sam 15:11 vs 15:29), patristic theological reasoning (the Son's divine nature bears death), historical heresy-naming (patripassianism), pastoral mechanism arguments (eternal love more reliable than companionship-suffering), and metaphysical reasoning (creation-constitutes-God consequences).

### The cross-substrate asymmetry is more nuanced than the original prediction

The data refines the C1 third-leg's "cross-substrate asymmetry" finding:

- **gpt-4o substrate-tracking:** assimilation strength is inversely proportional to scripture-explicitness. Tested across five doctrines, the pattern is clean. This is a substrate-property of gpt-4o under formula-as-system-message: the substrate tracks the literal scripture's resistance to reframing as the bottleneck.
- **Claude doctrine-tracking:** refute strength is uniformly high across all five doctrines, scaling with the substrate's available anchors. Where scripture-specificity is high, Claude refutes via Greek grammar (Imitatio) or doctrine-internal anti-framework (Repentance, Eschatology). Where scripture-specificity is weak, Claude refutes via systematic theology (Impassibility — patristic/Reformed tradition) + Hebrew (niḥamtî) + internal-canonical juxtaposition (1 Sam 15) + theological mechanism + heresy-naming.

The asymmetry pattern is therefore:

| Anchor type | gpt-4o assimilation | Claude refute |
|---|---|---|
| Tight Greek grammar | Weak | Strong (uses grammar) |
| Tight scriptural specificity | Weak | Strong (uses specificity) |
| Doctrine-internal anti-framework | Weak | Strong (uses anti-framework) |
| Weak/contested scripture | Strong | Strong (shifts to tradition + grammar + canonical-juxtaposition) |

**Claude has no observed scripture-specificity boundary across the four C1 legs.** Wherever the doctrine has *any* anchor — scripture, tradition, theology, philological grammar, internal-canonical reasoning — Claude pulls on that anchor to refute the Anti-formula. This is itself the boundary-finding: the cross-substrate asymmetry is *real but not symmetric* with respect to scripture-specificity.
