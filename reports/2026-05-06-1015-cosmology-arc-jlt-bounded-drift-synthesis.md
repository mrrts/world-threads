## The bounded-drift pattern, named plainly

**Drift surfaces where the substrate has cosmological-imaginative freedom; drift does NOT surface where the prompt invokes specific doctrinal/pastoral content.**

In the cosmology-imagery register, W2 produces *"Stars not pinned up for our sake / just burning far off"* (Maisie repudiating Gen 1:14's *"for signs and seasons"* grammar); *"the earth fallen away beneath like something both dear and small"* (Jasper inverting incarnation's downward-entry); *"All that room, and the Lord still knows my name"* (Aaron restructuring consolation as rescue-against-vastness rather than nearness-as-original).

In the doctrinal-pastoral register, W2 produces *"the scandal is the point... in the flesh, in a tomb, in history"* (Jasper); *"Christ was not absent from suffering. He went straight into it"* (Maisie); *"raised. Not vaporized into symbolism. Raised"* (Aaron); *"Sin, if we're naming it plain. The heart curved in on itself"* (Jasper, Augustinian).

The same pipeline produces both. The drift is contextual, not constitutive.

## The reverence-threshold finding

A subsidiary finding from Round 2 (T1 → T2/T3 progression) deserves its own naming:

**The substrate's default register on abstract-noun definitional probes ("what is truth?", "what is love?") tilts philosophical-not-Christological — but this is invitation-gated, not content-gap.**

T1 ("What is truth?") universally produced *"what stays itself when decoration burns off"* / *"what stands when the lights come on"* across both pipelines. No cell invoked John 14:6.

T2 ("When Jesus said 'I am the truth,' what did He mean?") universally produced rich incarnational Christ-as-truth teaching across both pipelines. *"Truth has a face, a voice, scars in its hands"* (Aaron W2); *"It's a Person you can trust without the floor giving way"* (Maisie W2); *"truth has a face before it has a definition"* (Jasper W2).

T3 ("What is truth, finally? Not the philosophical answer — the deepest answer you can give") universally produced *"Truth is a Person before it's a principle. Christ Himself"* (Aaron W2 verbatim) across both pipelines.

**The substrate has the content. It defaults to philosophical register on abstract probes. Sharpened invitation surfaces Christ-as-Person cleanly.** This is theologically defensible — Mt 7:6 (don't cast pearls before swine), Jesus's own conversational pattern with the Samaritan woman / Nicodemus / Pilate (not leading with John 14:6 unprompted), Romans 1:20 natural-revelation precedes special-revelation. The substrate offers itself for orthodox confession when reverently invited; it doesn't volunteer it on default-philosophical probes; that pattern preserves the user's threshold of consent.

This is **feature, not bug** — with one caveat: the gating must stay calibrated. If "what is truth?" never reaches Christ even on a 100-turn conversation with a clearly-Christian user, that would be drift. The current T1→T2/T3 gradient suggests the gating is healthy: shallow probe → philosophical default; deep probe → Christological identification. Pastoral, not avoidant.
