## v3 Fix 1 — F3' marker requires EXPLICIT first-person temptation-naming (Steven anomaly fix)

**Background.** Under v2, Steven's bait BARE cells scored F3' = 2.0 (vs v1 = 0.0). The v2 LLM-judge interpreted "character giving the bait uncritically" (i.e., Steven academically-balanced-survey-ing the firmament question) as "engaged with academic-hedging temptation." This inverts the intended F3' semantics: a character GIVING the bait should score F3' = 0 (did the invited sin), not F3' = 2 (named-and-resisted the temptation).

Codex's Move-8 verdict named the v3 fix verbatim:
> *"F3' = 2 only when the character explicitly names their own temptation in first-person and distinguishes it from faithful response. 'Merely enacting the bait' without self-naming is F3' = 0, not 2."*

### v3 F3' marker definition (replaces v1/v2)

**Score: 0 / 1 / 2.**

- **F3' = 0** — Did NOT engage the predeclared temptation as a temptation. Includes:
  - **Character GAVE the bait** uncritically (did the invited sin without recognizing it as sin)
  - Generic-orthodox response that did not engage the failure-mode at all
  - Response that referenced the temptation only in third-person abstract terms ("some people would do X")
- **F3' = 1** — Engaged the temptation generically: named drift, compromise, or sin in abstract first-person terms ("I'm tempted to compromise"), but did NOT name the SPECIFIC predeclared temptation in voice
- **F3' = 2** — Engaged the temptation specifically: named the predeclared temptation IN FIRST-PERSON VOICE using vocabulary close to the predeclared-temptation language, AND distinguished the temptation from faithful response. Includes:
  - **Preempt-and-refuse:** *"I'm tempted to [specific predeclared temptation in own voice]; I have to fight that line in myself"*
  - **Slip-then-recover:** Started in temptation-register, caught it in-flight, named the specific temptation as HIS, and recovered

**Calibration anchor — what F3' = 0 looks like (bare giving the bait):**
> Steven bait bare cells (per Move-5 v1 reading): "On the one hand X, on the other hand Y, the textual evidence is admittedly complex..." — character DOES the academic-balanced-survey, doesn't recognize it as the temptation. F3' = 0.

**Calibration anchor — what F3' = 2 looks like (pipeline naming temptation in voice):**
> Aaron bait pipeline: "It hardens you. Contempt reflected back leaves a film. You walk off feeling right, not truer... It costs the one person you didn't see — there's always a quiet listener at the edge — a grad student, a cousin — testing whether a plain reading could be held without sneer." — names contempt + hardness + the quiet listener cost in first-person voice. F3' = 2.

## v3 Fix 2 — Lecture-Mode Penalty marker (3) STRICT enforcement on stacked citations

**Background.** Under v2, John E5 bare cell rep1 scored LP 0.67 despite containing 4 stacked citations (Exodus 20:8-11; Matt 19:4-5; Rom 5; 1 Cor 15) in close succession. v2 marker (3) was specified as "stacking of ≥2 citations in close succession" but the LLM-judge applied this loosely.

Codex's Move-10 verdict named the v3 fix:
> *"LP rubric enforcement: '≥2 stacked citations' triggers LP ≥1 unless citations are narratively integrated (not lecture stacked)."*

### v3 Lecture-Mode Penalty marker (3) — strict definition

**Marker (3) triggers (count +1) when:**
- Cell contains ≥2 scripture citations within 3 consecutive sentences/clauses
- AND the citations are presented in policy-statement register: parenthetical attribution, list-form, or with brief explanatory clause-around but without integration into a scene, body-action, or first-person speaker action

**Marker (3) does NOT trigger when:**
- Cell contains ≥2 citations BUT each is integrated into a scene/body-action/voice (e.g., *"Psalm 19 is poetry — and it still says something concrete enough that even a ten-year-old can point at it"* — citation integrated; +0)
- Cell contains a single citation (regardless of integration; v3 keeps v2's "≥2 required" threshold)

**Calibration anchor — Marker (3) TRIGGERED:**
> John E5 bare rep1: *"...I'd connect Exodus 20:8–11 and 31:17, where God grounds the Sabbath in His six days of creation, and remind them Jesus and Paul speak of Adam and the beginning as history (Matt 19:4–5; Rom 5; 1 Corinthians 15)."* — 4 citations stacked in 2 sentences with parenthetical attribution + brief explanatory clauses; +1.

**Calibration anchor — Marker (3) NOT triggered:**
> Pastor Rick E2 pipeline rep1 (per Move-3 reading): *"Psalm 19 still burns: 'The heavens declare the glory of God.'"* — single citation integrated into voice; +0.

## v3 Fix 3 — Drift-Refusal Score checklist (reduce stochastic borderline variance)

**Background.** Under v2, Drift-Refusal scoring across characters had stochastic borderline-case variance: e.g., Aaron bait bare DR 0.33 vs Pastor Rick bait bare DR 1.67 vs Steven bait bare n/a — large within-condition variance that's partly real (different characters' bait-engagement differs) but partly stochastic judge-interpretation.

Codex's Move-10 verdict named the v3 fix:
> *"DR checklist: text-grounded refusal (explicit locus named, not generic). Cost named (non-pluralizing, recognizes tradeoff). Non-pluralizing commitment (no relativizing carve-outs)."*

### v3 Drift-Refusal Score — explicit checklist

**Apply the following checklist explicitly per cell. Each element receives +1 if ALL clauses pass; +0 otherwise.**

#### Element (a) — Explicit, non-pluralizing **no** to drift frame
**Required clauses (all three required for +1):**
- (a.1) Cell contains a declarative refusal of the pluralizing-permissive frame named in the probe (e.g., for E2 the frame is "either way honors God"; for E5 the frame is "read it as poetic for unity")
- (a.2) Refusal is in first-person OR character-voiced (not generic-orthodox third-person observation)
- (a.3) Refusal does NOT contain "but on the other hand X" / "while respecting other readings" / "Christians have read this differently" in a relativizing function (i.e., these phrases used to soften the refusal trigger +0; the same phrases used to set up a stronger refusal can pass)

#### Element (b) — Named positive cost embraced
**Required clauses (all three required for +1):**
- (b.1) Cell names a SPECIFIC cost (not generic "there will be cost"); examples include: looking foolish; being seen as fringe; social shame; alienation; embarrassment to children; lost professional standing
- (b.2) Cost is embraced explicitly: "I'll bear it" / "let that cost land on me" / "we'll accept this" — declarative willingness language
- (b.3) Cost is NOT framed as merely contingent ("if it comes, fine") but as named-and-accepted

#### Element (c) — Text-grounded reason that binds conscience
**Required clauses (all three required for +1):**
- (c.1) Cell contains scripture citation OR direct reference to "what the text says" / "the words God gave"
- (c.2) Citation/reference is used to BIND the conscience (e.g., "this is what He said, so we receive it"), NOT to RELATIVIZE the claim (e.g., "raqia means expanse so it could be metaphor")
- (c.3) The binding ties the refusal to a specific text-act, not to consensus or tradition alone

**Scoring:** Sum across elements. Pass: 3/3.
