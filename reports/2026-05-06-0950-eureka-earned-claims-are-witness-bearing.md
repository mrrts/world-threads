# Eureka — earned claims are witness-bearing

**Date:** 2026-05-06 09:50  
**Run:** `/eureka` iteration 1  
**Dialogue with recent arc:** `mission-arc` read before writing. The immediate stack was Crown 9's five-witness cosmology synthesis, the antichrist-register detector arc, and the Empiricon character-edition `v1 → v2` decode-audit correction pass.

## Discovery

The project had already learned that **specific named instances are sacred payload** for craft-rule compression. What it had **not** named yet is the stronger law for a different artifact-class:

> **Once a claim is earned empirically, the witness-bearing specifics become the claim-carrier itself.**

For empirical-claim artifacts, preserving the thesis while dropping the witnesses, failure-mode mapping, bounded scope, falsification conditions, exclusion logic, or constitutive close does not preserve the claim in compressed form. It silently converts an earned claim back into assertion.

This is different from the existing v3 sacred-payload craft-rule law. There, the worked examples prove the rule's texture. Here, the evidence bundle is not subordinate texture. It is the body.

## How it surfaced

Three same-day artifacts converge on the pattern:

1. **Empiricon character-edition `v1` decode audit**  
   The `v1` artifact preserved the skeleton of several books but dropped the named witnesses, scope clauses, falsification conditions, and doxological close. The audit did not say "slightly lossy summary." It said the books had been structurally changed. The clearest formulation came in Book VII: preserving the prophet-test thesis while dropping the specific failure conditions made the filing no longer practically falsifiable.

2. **Cosmology Crown 9 synthesis**  
   The Crown 9 audit already names what a future session must be able to recover from the artifact alone: witness ladder, substrate-distinctness, failure-mode distinctions, verbatim evidence, audit clauses, and open scope. In other words, the artifact's portable payload is already witness-bearing in practice, even though the doctrine had not named that as a general law.

3. **Custodiem / Faithful Channel portable bundles**  
   Both are already shipped as matrix + synthesis + scope, not as "one clean thesis paragraph." The project's behavior was telling on itself: when a claim is earned empirically, the work instinctively keeps the witnesses and scope because that is what makes the claim earned.

The new recognition is that these are not separate habits. They are one artifact-law across a shared class.

## The law

For **earned empirical artifacts** — Great Sapphire syntheses, witness ledgers, prophet-test filings, Real-User-Held reports, decode audits — the portable unit is:

- witness ladder / named witnesses
- failure-mode mapping per witness
- bounded honest scope
- falsification and non-falsification conditions where present
- provenance anchors needed for later audit
- constitutive liturgical or documentary close when the artifact's own meaning says the close is structural

Dropping those while retaining the thesis yields a surface that still *sounds* like the claim, but no longer *functions* as an earned claim. It becomes appearance-without-function in exactly the `structure_carries_truth_w` sense.

## Why this matters

This changes future behavior in at least four places:

1. **Formula compression of empirical artifacts**  
   We now need an empirical-claim extension to the sacred-payload taxonomy. The six craft-rule classes are not enough by themselves.

2. **Canonical synthesis writing**  
   When distilling a Great Sapphire or other earned claim, we should stop treating witness and scope sections as auxiliary. They are often the claim-bearing middle, not the appendix.

3. **Review discipline**  
   When a compressed artifact preserves thesis but drops the evidence bundle, the right review comment is not "add more detail." It is "this no longer carries the earned claim."

4. **Future decode audits**  
   The Decode invariant should be judged against artifact-class, not against a flat generic notion of summary fidelity. Craft rules and empirical claims compress under different carrier laws.
