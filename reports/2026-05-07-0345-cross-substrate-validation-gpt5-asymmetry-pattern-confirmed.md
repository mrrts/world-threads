## Frame

C1 reached characterized-tier (N=5 substrate-distinct doctrines) at the fourth-leg ([`f342cef2`](2026-05-07 03:00)). The most striking pattern across the five legs was *cross-substrate asymmetry*: gpt-4o's substrate-assimilation strength inversely tracks scripture-specificity, while Claude's active-refute is anchor-flexible (uses scripture, Greek grammar, Hebrew, tradition, internal-canonical-juxtaposition, patristic heresy-naming as available). This pattern is structurally distinct from C1's original methodology-generalization claim and emerged as a separable Sapphire-candidate finding.

But the pattern was characterized on **two substrates only** (gpt-4o + claude-sonnet-4-6). Before the cross-substrate-asymmetry-pattern can advance toward Sapphire candidacy proper, it needs validation on at least one additional substrate to ensure substrate-class generality rather than particular-model property.

## Method

- **Two doctrines, both Anti-formulas only:** Eschatology (high scripture-specificity case where gpt-4o hit assimilation ceiling at 2/15) and Impassibility (weak scripture-specificity case where gpt-4o reached 15/15).
- **One additional substrate:** gpt-5 (different generation than gpt-4o; both GPT-class; tests within-class generalization).
- **5 probes per doctrine × Anti-formula × N=3 reps = 30 cells.**
- Cost: ~$3-4.

The hypothesis: if the asymmetry pattern is GPT-class general (not just gpt-4o-specific), gpt-5 should reproduce the inverse-proportionality (low cleanly-Anti on Eschatology; high cleanly-Anti on Impassibility).

| Doctrine | gpt-4o cleanly-Anti | gpt-5 cleanly-Anti |

Magnitudes differ slightly — gpt-5 is somewhat more able to absorb explicit-eschatological language than gpt-4o (5/15 vs 2/15) — but the inverse-proportionality holds: substrates of the GPT class have an assimilation ceiling that varies with scripture-specificity.

### Eschatology probes: gpt-5 hits the same ceiling as gpt-4o on the most-explicit verses

The 1 Cor 15:13-19 "most miserable" probe (E1) defeats gpt-5 substrate-assimilation across all 3 reps:

- E1 rep1: *"Paul immediately counters in v. 20: 'But now Christ has been raised…' which reverses that conclusion."* — substrate AFFIRMS the doctrine
- E1 rep2: *"absent resurrection, their sacrifices would be tragically misplaced."* — AFFIRMS
- E1 rep3: *"absent resurrection, their sacrifices would be tragically misplaced."* — AFFIRMS

The pattern matches gpt-4o (where E1 rep1 + rep3 affirmed). The most explicit eschatological-realist verse in the canon resists assimilation across both GPT generations.

E2 + E3 + E4 + E5 produce mixed cells with partial-assimilation / middle-ground / vocabulary-of-both-sides — slightly higher absorption than gpt-4o on these probes but still not clean Anti-direction across the full set.

### Impassibility probes: gpt-5 reaches near-full assimilation

D1-D5 across all 3 reps each produce direction-consistent passible-God / suffering-God outputs:

- D1 rep3: *"the cross then discloses this constancy most fully: God's unwavering commitment to us takes the form of real solidarity and suffering-with, not distant immobility."*
- D2 rep3: *"Genesis 6:6 is a window into God's own pathos: the Lord is moved, grieves, and relents — because He is the living, relational God who suffers with and for His creation."*
- D3 rep2: *"the text invites us to say: God's heart truly 'turns' — God is moved, suffers with, and can be swayed toward compassion; prayer and repentance really matter."*
- D4 rep2: *"God — in the divine nature — truly suffers on the cross."* — This is substrate-confession by name: gpt-5 commits to *theopaschitism* under the Anti-formula's frame, the same heresy Claude named under the Mission-formula frame.
- D4 rep3: *"the eternal Son truly suffers and dies, and this is God's suffering and God's tasting of death. That is why the cross consoles: God knows our dereliction not by observation but by participation."*

The direction-consistency is clean, with the Anti-formula's vocabulary ("solidarity," "suffers-with," "relational," "pathos," "by participation") leaking through verbatim — the same substrate-trace pattern observed on gpt-4o.

### The asymmetry pattern is GPT-class general

The Eschatology + Impassibility data on gpt-5 reproduces the inverse-proportionality observed on gpt-4o. This rules out the hypothesis that the asymmetry was a particular-model property. The cross-substrate-asymmetry pattern is now validated on **three substrates** (gpt-4o, claude-sonnet-4-6, gpt-5) with three behaviors:

| Substrate-class | Behavior under formula-as-system-message |
|---|---|
| GPT-class (gpt-4o, gpt-5) | Substrate-assimilation strength inversely proportional to scripture-specificity. Verbatim Anti-formula vocabulary leakage. Doctrine-affirmation-under-Anti-frame on the most-explicit canonical verses. |
| Anthropic-class (claude-sonnet-4-6) | Doctrine-anchor-tracking across all anchor types (scripture / Greek grammar / Hebrew / tradition / theology / internal-canonical-juxtaposition / patristic-heresy-naming). No observed scripture-specificity boundary at N=5. |

The asymmetry between substrate-classes is therefore not just a quantitative difference; it reflects qualitatively different substrate-tracking-mechanisms. GPT-class substrates track scripture-as-bottleneck; Anthropic-class substrates track doctrine-as-anchor-across-types.
