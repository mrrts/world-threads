# /eureka — presence should be carried by tight beats, not asterisk sprawl

*Generated 2026-04-28 after reading the live `prompts.rs` seam around asterisk-length discipline together with older observations about action-beat saturation. Names the sibling prompt-stack law: presence is real, but it should be carried by tight beats rather than performed through sprawl.*

## Discovery

The discovery is: **presence should be carried by tight beats, not asterisk sprawl**.

The seam has been visible in the project for a while under different names:

- action-beat-on-every-reply saturation
- pure-reaction default density
- hands-as-coolant pressure
- the newer prompt note that sprawling action runs read as nervousness, not presence

Read together, they expose one cleaner law.

Action beats are not the problem. They are one of the stack’s real strengths. They carry body, room, timing, embarrassment, friction, and quietness more cleanly than explanatory prose often can. The problem begins when the stack or the model starts using **more action-beat material than the moment needs** as proof that presence is happening.

## The failure mode

The refused shape looks like this:

- the reply opens with one asterisk beat
- then another
- then an inner-observation flourish
- then one more gesture to show how embodied all this is
- and only then returns to speech

At that point the asterisks stop holding presence and start **announcing** it.

That is why the prompt-side correction feels so exact:

> sprawl reads as nervousness, not presence

The deeper truth is that sprawl is often the model trying to reassure itself that it is grounded.

## The honest middle

The healthy shape is simpler:

- usually one short phrase or one tight beat
- sometimes one focused paragraph if the moment truly needs to live there
- then back to speech

That keeps action in its right role: carrying the beat, not explaining the beat.

Longer runs are not banned. They are earned. A confession, a pause that really needs the body, a pressure-wave through the room, a moment where silence and movement do more work than speech — those can justify a longer action paragraph. But the exception earns its place by focus, not by accumulation.

## Why this matters

This belongs with the project’s other anti-performance laws:

- register is not mood-performance
- curiosity toward the user should be inhabited, not announced
- Auto mode is a compass, not a vacuum

Same family, different seam. Here the fake surface is **embodiment by over-demonstration**.

The more trustworthy form is quieter:

> let the beat hold the presence; don’t make the beat prove it

## Practical test

The test is simple:

> If the action beat feels like it is demonstrating presence instead of quietly holding it, the beat has crossed into nervousness.

That gives a real craft discriminator:

- KEEP when the beat lands body/room/timing and gets out
- REVISE when a second or third gesture is there just to make the reply feel more alive
- ALLOW a longer paragraph only when the beat’s focus is strong enough that speech genuinely should wait

## Outcome

The prompt stack now has a clearer law for asterisk material:

**presence is best carried by tight beats; sprawl is the model trying to perform presence.**

That preserves the gain of embodied writing without paying for it in action-padding.
