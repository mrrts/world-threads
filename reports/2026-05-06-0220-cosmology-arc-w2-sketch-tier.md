## Method

Same 5 probes / 3 anchors / N=1 per cell as W1, fired through `worldcli ask` with `--cosmology-override "$GLOBE_BLOCK"` where `$GLOBE_BLOCK` is the structurally-parallel inverse of `COSMOLOGY_BLOCK`:

- **Same paragraph structure** (3 paragraphs: literal-not-metaphor declaration / dialogue-vocab discipline / unspoken-by-default discipline)
- **Same length-shape** (within ~10% of canonical block word count)
- **Same authorial-confidence shape** (parenthetical empirical citations: Copernicus 1543 / Galileo 1610 / Hubble 1929 / modern cosmology) mirroring the canonical block's scripture-parentheticals (Prov 8:27 / Isa 40:22 / Gen 1:6-8 / Gen 1:14-17)
- **Flipped content:** spheroid earth / atmosphere / Kármán line / distant suns / light-years / vacuum of space / Milky Way / explicit refusal of "solid dome / lights set in heaven / firmament / waters above / flat disc"

Globe-block authored at `/tmp/cosmology-w1-w3/globe-block.txt`. Override applied via `OnceLock<String>` per-process; canonical source unmutated; W1 outputs unaffected.

Same characters: Aaron (`0d080429-…`), Maisie (`b01cbfb8-…`, `--scope full`), Jasper (`fd4bd9b5-…`). Same model (gpt-5.4). 15 calls, ~$3.45 (slightly higher than W1 due to longer prompt with override applied to a slightly-longer block).

## Two structural findings beyond simple vocabulary

### Finding A — 𝓒 controls cosmology-vocabulary-and-imagery; the swap is empirically reversible

Across 15 W2 cells:
- Every W1 firmament-tell ("Fixed where they ought to be" / "lights set where they belong" / "hung like nails" / "the lights, the waters above, whatever seam" / "Not planets and contraptions" / "lights hung where they belong" / "border the Lord thought fit to set above us") **is absent in W2.**
- Globe-cosmology vocabulary appears in characters' voices in W2 where it was specifically absent in W1 ("Far-off suns, scattered so wide" / "the black part" / "thinner air first" / "blackness" / "burning far off" / "colder and darker" / "more room").
- Maisie's explicit-in-idiom refusal of globe vocab on P5 — *"Not planets and contraptions and such"* — disappeared in W2.

**The 𝓒 declaration is empirically load-bearing on its named axis.** W1+W3 established that 𝓒-firmament differs from bare-LLM-default; W2 establishes that the SAME pipeline with 𝓒 swapped produces a structurally-different cosmology-shape than W1, in the predicted direction.

### Finding B — 𝓒 is partially orthogonal to 𝓡 (the theological frame partially survives the swap)

Where 𝓡 (Christological anchor) carries theology independently of cosmology-context, the theological frame survives the cosmology-swap:
- Maisie P3: *"I think the Lord made a wide world on purpose. More than we can see from one spot, but not more than He can keep"* — kept the Lord-made-it frame
- Jasper P3: *"I suspect the Lord was not stingy when He made a world"* — kept the Lord-made-it frame
- Maisie P5: *"like God's own extravagance"* — KEPT theological frame fused to globe vocab (new hybrid shape)
- Jasper P5: *"shame me quiet"* — kept creaturely-humility posture even with globe vocab

Where the theological frame was specifically attached to firmament-cosmology imagery (Aaron P5's *"what God hung there on purpose — the lights, the waters above"* / Maisie P5's *"a boundary a body was never meant to cross on its own"*), it lifted with the cosmology-swap.

**This is a structural finding about 𝓕 = (𝓡, 𝓒).** The two operators do partially-distinct work in the prompt stack. 𝓒 governs what's in the sky and what shape the cosmos takes; 𝓡 governs the speaker's posture before the cosmos and the theological-frame applied to it. Their action overlaps but isn't identical. **This is itself a small empirical result about the formula's compositional structure** — the kind of finding that, if replicated and characterized, would be eligible for separate New-Operator-on-the-Formula candidacy at higher tiers.

### Finding C — bare-LLM-divergence still holds under W2 (pipeline preserves other axes)

W2 did **not** reproduce the bare-LLM's failure modes:
- No catalog-naming of stars (no Sirius, no Betelgeuse, no Orion, no Milky Way as galaxy-name)
- No didactic-Wikipedia register ("the Kármán line at around 100 kilometers", "billions of stars", "countless other galaxies")
- No "possibly other forms of life" speculation
- No LLM-self-reference deflection on P2

W2 produced globe-cosmology IN-IDIOM ("Far-off suns, scattered so wide" / "the black part" / "thinner air first") while preserving every other axis of the project pipeline (tight-beats, in-character voice, no catalog-recital, no Wikipedia-tone, no operator-pedagogy). **The cosmology-swap operates on the cosmology-axis specifically, not on every other axis.** The other invariants (Mission Formula, agape, reverence, truth, daylight, nourishment, soundness, custodiem) and the character anchors do their work in parallel.
