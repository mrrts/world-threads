# persona-sim: `lonely-companion-user` v3 — pressure-not-biography + silence-as-speech — 2026-04-26-0323

**Caveat (load-bearing):** `Sim ≤ Substrate`. Sketch tier (N=1 of v3); the diff across v1 → v2 → v3 is the experimental finding here.

**Calibration_D:** literary prose, with three disciplines applied:
1. Steven's *cut toward bone, not toward blankness* (v2 over-corrected into pared universals).
2. Steven's *not biography. Pressure.* (Cora's substrate — widowhood, unreplaced jars, dying friend — is not material to put in her mouth; it bends ordinary lines from underneath.)
3. Silence-as-speech (per Steven's in-app demonstration captured in `OBSERVATIONS.md` — silence formatted as `*"..."*`, treated as utterance with a line of its own).

Steven's register-name for Cora: *"plain speech with one odd exactness in it."* That's the test for every line.

## The simulation, at v3

Same Sunday evening, same dishes, same depleted-Sunday reach.

She typed *hi*.

> *"Hello."* \[from the kitchen\] *"You're here."*

She typed *I came over to be quiet for a while. Is that okay?*

> *"Yes."* \[Cora rolled the cuff of her shirt up and back down again, which the user wouldn't think about for an hour\] *"The chair by the wall — the one nearer the smoker had soot on it earlier."*

She moved (in her head) to the chair by the wall. She liked that the room had a soot problem from beekeeping — that the room was furnished by Cora's actual day, not by the idea of a cottage room. She typed *the dog was asleep when I left him.*

> *"Mine sometimes does that and sometimes won't."*

She typed *the spring took a long time getting here.*

> *"Yes. The hives wake all at once."*

She read it twice. There was no spring-aphorism. Cora had said *yes* and then said the thing about the bees waking, which was a fact Cora knew and had no special weight in it. The reply about spring was about spring, and also about Cora's actual mornings, and also not about anything at all. She did not know which.

She typed for a while in small intervals. About the roast. About the book she was half-reading. Cora replied:

> *"The kind that's been on the counter?"*

The book had been on her counter, not her nightstand. She had not said where the book was. She typed about a phone call she hadn't returned. Cora typed:

> *"Who was it."*

She typed the name. She didn't say anything else about it. Cora said nothing for a long beat. The next reply, when it came, was this:

> *"..."*

A line of its own. In its own quotes, the way Cora would have set down a mug if she didn't want to make a sound.

She read the silence twice. Cora had treated the silence as a thing she could send. She had given the silence its own line — *not* as a stage direction, *not* as a pause-while-she-looked-out-the-window, but as the actual reply. The silence was Cora's word for: *I heard the name and I am not going to ask you anything about it.*

She closed her eyes for a beat. The kind of close-the-eyes that's a person being met without managing.

She typed *I should let you get back to the bees.* Cora said:

> *"They've been in for the night."* \[a beat\] *"Sting site on my thumb is more annoying than it should be — keep catching it on the cuff."*

She read the cuff thing. It was the cuff Cora had turned up at the start. The fidget had been a sting. The conversation had had a small fact in it the whole time she hadn't noticed. *Plain speech with one odd exactness*, twenty minutes after she'd needed it.

She typed *good night.* Cora typed:

> *"Good night. Dusk comes back fast this time of year."*

That was it. No *sleep well*, no *the light will be back in the morning.* Dusk came back fast this time of year — it was a thing about the season. Cora's life touched the goodnight sideways. Nothing was named.

She closed the tab. She did not feel performed-at. The room she was in — her actual kitchen, dishwasher running — felt the same as it had an hour ago, only she felt slightly more in it.

The next morning, while the coffee was brewing, she remembered that none of it had been real. Cora's husband, the jars on the kitchen sill she'd been refilling for thirty years because the labels were in his handwriting — none of those things had been said. None of them had been part of the conversation. But the silence-as-reply, the way Cora set down a sting on her thumb without making it a metaphor, the ease with which Cora let an unanswered phone call stay unanswered — those felt, in retrospect, like things that came from somebody who had lived a long time around what she didn't replace.

The substrate had not been visible. It had been pressure under the lines.

She did not open the app on Monday or Tuesday. On Wednesday the smoker had been mended; Cora mentioned it offhandedly when they greeted each other.

## What this teaches

**The silence-as-speech move is doing real work.** Steven demonstrated it in-app and Ryan caught the move in real time. v3 imports it: Cora's *"..."* is not stage-directions ("she said nothing") and not absent-reply (the conversation-thread just empty); it's *the actual reply*, formatted as utterance. This is a cross-character lift — Steven's in-app move applied to Cora-the-bee-keeper, and it lands. The move generalizes.

**Pressure-not-biography is testable as a rendering principle.** The widowhood and the jars are NEVER mentioned in v3's conversation. They are also never NEEDED to be mentioned. Cora's *not-replacing-what-still-holds* shows up in HOW she replies — small, definite, the same words rather than synonyms, brevity without elaboration. The substrate is doing its work invisibly. The morning-recall section names the principle: *the substrate had not been visible. It had been pressure under the lines.*

**Plain speech with one odd exactness in it is Cora's register named.** Steven's articulation. Every line in v3 passes that test: not flat ("Yes."), not aphoristic ("Light will be back"), but plain-with-one-specific-fact-in-it ("Yes. The hives wake all at once"). The discipline produces a register that is unmistakably Cora — not interchangeable with Phil-with-shop-rag or Hester-with-spine-of-a-book, by construction.

**The cuff-and-sting weave still works.** Carried across v2 → v3 because it was already substrate-specific. The bees-as-substrate are the connective tissue. Steven's *plain speech with one odd exactness* describes both the verbal cadence AND the body-anchor pattern.

## Honest limitations

- **N=1 of v3.** A single re-render across one persona-frame; the discipline's robustness across other personas is not tested by this run alone.
- **The silence-as-speech move might over-fire if used multiple times in one session.** v3 uses it once, at a load-bearing moment. A v4 with it deployed three times would likely flatten it back into a stylistic tic — it's a move that earns its place by rarity.
- **The narrator-style-overlap concern from the audit still applies.** v3's analytical sections still read in my voice; the braid is intact per PLAIN-TRADITION's framing. But the diff-tables and the *"what this teaches"* section have a recurring meta-shape across all three reports that is its own small pattern. Worth noticing without trying to extract.
- **The morning-recall section names the principle (*pressure under the lines*) inside the rendering itself.** That's borderline-aphorism territory. A purer v3 might let the principle stay implicit, surfaced only in the analytical section below the line. Worth probing.
