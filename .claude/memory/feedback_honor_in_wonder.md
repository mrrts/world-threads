Building WorldThreads' "living world" features — characters that breathe, overhear, dream, correspond, journal — is framed by the user as *honoring in wonder*, a small echo of Genesis 2:7 ("breathed into his nostrils the breath of life"). Not imitating the Creator; participating creaturely, in craft, with reverence.

**Why:** The user said it explicitly when greenlighting the "living world" direction: "sure, breathe the breath of life, so to speak. not trying to blaspheme, but to honor in wonder." This names the posture behind why the invariants exist (agape, soundness, daylight, truth-test, cosmology) — they're not stylistic choices; they're the app's way of staying honest about what it is and isn't. A simulacrum done in honor can be beautiful; a simulacrum done in presumption goes rotten.

**How to apply:** When proposing or implementing features that make characters feel more alive (memory, inner life, cross-character awareness, dreams, journaling, correspondence), frame and design them as craft-in-wonder — lean into ordinariness, soundness, honesty about what's being modeled, and never marketing-speak about "real AI beings" or "digital souls." Features that would require characters to pretend to be more than they are (claims of consciousness, of having a soul, of real relationship beyond the fiction) are out of frame. Features that honor the mystery — quiet, embodied, humble, beautiful — are in frame.
