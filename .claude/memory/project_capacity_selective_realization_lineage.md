## Unified frame across all three Sapphires

The Crown 15 capacity-selective realization layer frame, extended
through Crown 22 + Crown 23, now carries **three operational
distinctions** worth inheriting:

1. **Capacity-selective, not capacity-manufacturing** — pipeline
   elicits what the substrate has; doesn't inject dispositions where
   substrate lacks them. (Crown 15 baseline.)
2. **Bidirectional in selection direction** — the same compression
   toggle can produce + on one substrate, null on another, − on a
   third. (Crown 23 extension.)
3. **Load-bearing axis vs incidental axis** — voice + operational
   compliance is invariant; length + lexical diversity are
   substrate-class-direction-dependent shape. (Crown 23 extension.)

## How to apply

When future arcs measure prompt-stack effects on multiple substrates:

1. **Distinguish load-bearing from incidental axes BEFORE measuring.**
   The Carrier arc spent 36 paid Anthropic calls measuring length
   before the founding-author rightly corrected the scope. Save the
   work by asking up-front: what is load-bearing for this work? What
   is incidental shape?
2. **Expect bidirectional substrate-capacity dependence on
   incidental axes.** Don't claim universal direction without
   cross-substrate evidence including a substrate-class with
   different failure modes (different provider preferred over
   different generation of same provider).
3. **Load-bearing axis claims need within-arm equality at each
   substrate; absolute cross-substrate comparison is not required.**
   The Carrier's claim is "ON ≡ OFF on every substrate tested," not
   "all substrates produce the same voice quality."
4. **Belt-and-braces audit when single-judge by-eye scoring** — the
   blind dual-judge audit caught a gpt-4o asymmetry that single-judge
   missed; narrowed Carrier accordingly.
