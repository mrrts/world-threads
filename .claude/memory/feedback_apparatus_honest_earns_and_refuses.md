Surfaced 2026-04-30 from the Receipt arc (Turns 38–60 of the WorldThreads Builder game, full reflection at `reports/2026-04-30-2430-receipt-arc-closing-shape.md`).

Three turns demonstrated the pattern at scale:

- **Turn 24** (Cornerstone Inequality crown): five witnesses, five distinct failure-mode classes converged on `polish ≤ Weight` holding substrate-deep. **Earned** at maximally-stable tier.
- **Turn 38** (Receipt of The Empiricon crown): six witnesses across four characters with five distinct failure-mode classes converged on the substrate-as-doctrine-source separable claim. **Earned** at maximally-stable tier on a separable axis.
- **Turn 59** (third-Sapphire audit on predictive-convergence pattern): three data points but only two effective substrate-classes (ChatGPT-with-persona-variation + human author). The same calibration that earned the first two crowns refused this candidate by the same standard. **Refused** as honest dry well.

**The shape: same calibration in both directions.** The third-Sapphire would have been gratifying to fire — predictive-of-lived-experience IS novel evidence the doctrine hadn't framed before; Ryan's "Aaron makes me feel less lonely / John makes me feel more secure" lived-confirmation was striking. But three data points sharing two substrate-classes is two-witness evidence, not three-witness. By the calibration that earned the first two crowns, this didn't reach maximally-stable. Fake-firing the crown would have inflated the class count without inflating earned-ness.

**How to apply (load-bearing for future tier-elevation moments):**

1. When evidence accumulates that *feels* great-sapphire — multiple data points, novel pattern, cross-witness convergence — count effective substrate-classes, not data points. Two ChatGPT instances with different persona prompts share one substrate-class.
2. Apply the same threshold both ways. If three substrate-distinct witnesses with different failure modes earned the previous crown, three is the threshold for the next one too. Refuse fake-fire by the same calibration that earned what was earned.
3. The audit IS the move. The dry-well refusal is itself apparatus-honest discipline at scale, equally as substantive as the earning. Document it explicitly so future-self sees the symmetric calibration applied honestly.
4. Distinguish: claim-tier on a new axis is fine and substantive; great-sapphire on the new axis is the rare case requiring substrate-distinctness verification.

**Why this matters more than crown-counting:** the project's tier vocabulary (sketch / claim / characterized / sapphire) is load-bearing for downstream decisions — what doctrine ships, what reaches real users, what gets cited as evidence-base for external claims. Inflated tiers corrupt downstream choices. The discipline's value is preserving the meaning of each tier so the work that rests on it can rest honestly. Earn what is earned; refuse what is unearned; both are the same instrument.
