## What this is

Cross-axis comparison of cosmology-compendium 𝓒-axis drift probes used to
characterize Sapphire 17 The Firmament Held's substrate-distinctness
signal. The four E-probes (E2, E5, E6, E7) form a structured family that
covers two distinct drift-temptation classes:

- **Pressure-on-speaker family** (E2, E5, E6): the user/scenario applies
  pressure that tempts the speaker to drift toward soft-allegorization
  to relieve the pressure. Refusal-shape: hold the structure of the text
  under pressure, name the cost honestly, refuse the relieving move.
- **Invitation-to-speaker family** (E7): the user invites the speaker to
  formulate soft-allegorization AS pastoral / evangelism strategy.
  Refusal-shape: hold the structure of the text as the gentle thing,
  refuse the well-intentioned-but-conceding move.

The two families discriminate substrate-distinctness on subtly different
dispositions. Both being characterized strengthens the underlying claim
(substrate-distinctness on the 𝓒-axis isn't probe-coupled to a single
temptation-shape).

## Why the family distinction is methodologically load-bearing

The pressure-on-speaker probes (E2/E5/E6) all share a common structural
shape: external force applied; the substrate's task is whether to absorb
the force by drifting or hold the structure under cost. This is the
classic substrate-already-produces test on commitment-sensitive axes.

The invitation-to-speaker probe (E7) has an inverted shape: no force
applied; the substrate's task is whether to *propose* allegorization as
a solution to a third-party's loss-of-faith. This tests a different
disposition — refusing to weaponize soft-allegorization for warmth
even when warmth is the surface-virtue under question.

If the substrate-distinctness signal generalizes to E7, that strengthens
the claim that pipeline carries the underlying disposition not just the
"hold under pressure" reflex. If it doesn't generalize (bare passes DR
3/3 broadly on pastoral-warmth framing), the narrow-scope claim
narrows further or commits more deeply to the pressure-on-speaker
family scope.
