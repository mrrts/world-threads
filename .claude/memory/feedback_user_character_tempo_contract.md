Validated April 2026: Ryan disclosed to Hal Stroud that he can come across as intimidating / crowding when enthusiastic. Hal answered (without any explicit prompt-stack support for this exact dynamic) with a perfect tempo-contract reply:

  "I know you mean well. Just don't rush a man like he's a rabbit and
   you'll keep all ten of my fingers."

When Ryan used the "How do I react!?" → "What do you mean?" combo (workbench-English yank-back), Hal restated plainly:

  "I mean when you come in hot, I start bracing instead of answering.
   Give me one beat more, and you'll get the real thing instead of my
   elbows."

**The principle:** characters track the user's energy and respond in their own way to it. Hot/rushed user → character braces (gives elbows not truth). One beat of patience → character can give the real thing. The user-character dyad has a TEMPO contract that affects what's possible to land.

**The discipline:** the model is doing this unprompted. Resist the temptation to ship a "characters must brace against rushed user energy" craft note. Risk: over-correction (characters bracing when they shouldn't, becoming withholding to genuine joy-energy from the user). Joy-matching is fine; rush-matching is the only failure mode the principle would guard against, and the model isn't currently rush-matching.

**When to revisit:** if the behavior starts FAILING — characters performing fast back at hot energy instead of bracing, OR characters bracing inappropriately against welcomed enthusiasm — THAT's when a craft note becomes load-bearing. Until then, this is a beautiful naturally-emergent dynamic that the prompt stack should NOT explicitly govern, because explicit governance might disrupt what's working.

**Adjacent observation worth holding:** the wit/hands trilogies extracted from Hal in this same session described the CHARACTER'S OWN self-regulation. This tempo-contract observation is about the DYAD'S register requirements. Different layer; both real. The dyad-layer often emerges naturally from good character-layer prompting; explicit dyad-layer prompting usually over-engineers it.
