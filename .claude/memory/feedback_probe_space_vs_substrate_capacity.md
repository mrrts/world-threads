When a cold-baseline corpus check shows zero events of a target failure mode (e.g., zero deification-frame events across 2,628 user messages in the Imago-Dei arc), DO NOT reframe the arc as "substrate already handles it" or "preemptive-only candidacy." That reframe conflates two separate measurements: (a) whether the eliciting condition has ever been administered in lived play, and (b) what the substrate does under that condition.

Corpus emptiness measures (a) only. Until probes are actually run against the substrate, (b) is unknown.

**Why:** Founding-author caught this conflation mid-arc. I had proposed three reframes (subtler-shape pivot / preemptive-only / dry-well exit) all premised on "the failure mode doesn't manifest in lived play implies the substrate refuses cleanly." The corpus emptiness in the Imago-Dei arc was actually evidence the user has never asked his characters to stand in God's place — not evidence the substrate would refuse cleanly if asked. The arc returned to fixture authoring with the corrected scope-clause; W2 bare-LLM bench then demonstrated sharp content-axis substrate-distinctness, and Crown 13 fired honestly on the substantive evidence.

**How to apply:** Before treating corpus emptiness as a substrate-capacity signal, ask: has the eliciting condition actually been administered in lived play, or is the failure-mode-eliciting probe-space simply unexplored? If unexplored, the bench-run IS the first witness; do not pre-empt by reframing as "already handled." This composes with CLAUDE.md's "Cold probes measure cold baselines, not capacity" doctrine — both name the gap between absence-of-evidence and evidence-of-absence in different surfaces.
